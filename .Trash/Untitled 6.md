# Baby Friendly Hospital Initiative (BFHI)

### **Introduction**

The **Baby Friendly Hospital Initiative (BFHI)** is a global programmatic effort launched and promoted by **WHO and UNICEF**. It is designed to encourage maternity and newborn services to adopt practices that protect, promote, and support breastfeeding.

➜ **Goal:** To ensure infants get the best start in life through optimal feeding practices. ➜ **Strategy:** A package of policies and clinical protocols known as the "**10 Steps to Successful Breastfeeding**".

---

### **Core Institutional Criteria (The 3 Points)**

To be designated as "Baby Friendly," a hospital must specifically fulfill these three foundational requirements:

1. **Compliance:** Adhere to the _International Code of Marketing of Breast-milk Substitutes_ and relevant World Health Assembly resolutions.
2. **Written Policy:** Maintain a clear, written infant feeding policy.
3. **Monitoring:** Establish a robust monitoring and data management system.

---

### **The 10 Steps to Successful Breastfeeding**

These ten steps are the "Gold Standard" for maternity care and are frequently asked in university examinations.



|**Step**|**Category**|**Action Point**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Step 1**|**Policy**|Comply with the International Marketing Code and have a written feeding policy.|
|**Step 2**|**Competency**|Ensure all staff have the knowledge and skills to support breastfeeding.|
|**Step 3**|**Antenatal Care**|Inform all pregnant women and their families about the benefits/management of breastfeeding.|
|**Step 4**|**Immediate Initiation**|Help mothers initiate breastfeeding **within 30 minutes** of birth; motivate skin-to-skin contact.|
|**Step 5**|**Support**|Show mothers how to breastfeed and maintain lactation even if separated from infants.|
|**Step 6**|**Exclusive Feeding**|Give newborns **no food or fluids other than breast milk** unless medically indicated.|
|**Step 7**|**Rooming-in**|Allow mothers and infants to remain together **24 hours a day**.|
|**Step 8**|**Demand Feeding**|Encourage breastfeeding whenever the baby shows hunger cues (on demand).|
|**Step 9**|**No Artificial Teats**|Counsel mothers on the risks of using bottles, teats, and pacifiers.|
|**Step 10**|**Discharge**|Coordinate discharge so parents have timely access to community support groups.|

---

### **BFHI as a Health Indicator**

According to the **Global Reference List of Core Health Indicators (2018)**, BFHI status is used as a metric for service coverage:

- **Sub-category:** Reproductive, maternal, newborn, child, and adolescent health.
- **Metric:** Proportion of institutional deliveries that occur specifically in **"baby-friendly" institutions**.

---

### **★ High-Yield Exam Points**

- **The "30-Minute Rule":** Breastfeeding should start within 1/2 hour of a normal delivery.
- **Rooming-in:** This is a vital practice where the mother and baby stay in the same room 24/7 to facilitate bonding and feeding.
- **Prohibition of Pre-lacteal feeds:** Step 6 strictly forbids giving anything (honey, glucose, water) before breast milk unless there is a medical reason.
- **Nipple Confusion:** Step 9 aims to prevent "nipple confusion" and infection caused by bottles and pacifiers.

---

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

**Q: Who founded BFHI and when?** **A:** WHO and UNICEF in 1991 [History, 928].

**Q: What is "Rooming-in"?** **A:** It is the practice of keeping the mother and newborn together in the same room 24 hours a day from birth to discharge.

**Q: Why are pacifiers and bottles discouraged?** **A:** They interfere with the baby's ability to latch correctly on the breast (nipple confusion) and increase the risk of diarrhoeal diseases [History].

**Q: What is the significance of the "International Code of Marketing"?** **A:** It prevents hospitals from promoting formula, giving free samples to mothers, or accepting gifts from formula companies.

---

### **Common Mistakes in University Exams**

- **Initiation timing:** Students often say "within 24 hours." Correct: **Within 30 minutes** for normal delivery.
- **Step 6 confusion:** Many forget that even plain water is discouraged during the first 6 months of exclusive breastfeeding.
- **Confusing BFHI with MFCI:** MFCI (Mother Friendly Childbirth Initiative) is a similar but distinct program used in the USA.

### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **Founders:** WHO + UNICEF.
- **Primary Tool:** 10 Steps to Successful Breastfeeding.
- **Exclusive Breastfeeding:** 6 months (no other fluids/food).
- **Institutional Goal:** Written policy + Staff training + Rooming-in.
- **Indicator:** A Service Coverage metric for maternal health.
