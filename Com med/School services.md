[[PM in Paediatrics]]
[[RBSK]]
The **School Health Programme**, primarily implemented in India under the **Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)** and as part of the broader **National Health Mission (NHM)**, is a specialized health service designed to promote, protect, and restore the health of school-age children.

The school years (6–18 years) are a period of rapid ==physical== and ==mental development==, making schools an ideal setting for health interventions that have lifelong benefits.

---

### **I. Objectives of School Health Services** ★ Important Exam Point

The ultimate goal is to ensure that children are healthy enough to participate effectively in education and lead productive lives.

1. **Promotion of Health:** To inculcate healthy lifestyle habits (hygiene, diet, exercise) at a young age.
2. **Prevention of Disease:** To prevent common childhood illnesses through immunization and nutritional interventions.
3. **Early Detection:** To screen for the "4Ds": **D**efects at birth, **D**eficiencies, **D**iseases, and **D**evelopmental delays.
4. **Early Intervention:** To provide timely management and referral for detected conditions, preventing long-term disability.
5. **Mental Well-being:** To identify ==emotional and behavioral== problems through a supportive teacher-pupil environment.

---

### **II. Components of the School Health Programme**

The program is comprehensive and multidimensional, involving various aspects of the child's life.

#### **1. Health Appraisal (Screening for 4Ds)** ★ Important Exam Point

This is the core activity under **RBSK**. Dedicated Mobile Health Teams screen school-age children (6–18 years) for:

- **Defects at birth:** e.g., Congenital heart disease, Down's syndrome.
- **Deficiencies:** e.g., Anaemia (combated via Weekly Iron-Folic Acid Supplementation), Vitamin A deficiency.
- **Diseases:** e.g., Skin conditions, Leprosy (integrated for early case detection), respiratory infections.
- **Developmental Delays:** e.g., Vision impairment, hearing loss, learning disabilities.

#### **2. School Eye Health Services (NPCB)**

- **Target:** Middle school children (10–14 years).
- **Activities:** Screening for refractive errors, squint, and amblyopia.
- **Mechanism:** Trained school teachers perform initial screening (one teacher per 150 students); children with vision **< 6/9** are referred to Ophthalmic Assistants.

#### **3. Nutritional Services**

- **Mid-day Meal Programme:** Provides nutritional support to primary and secondary students to improve attendance and combat malnutrition.
- **WIFS (Weekly Iron and Folic Acid Supplementation):** Provided to adolescents to prevent nutritional anaemia.
- **Iodized Salt:** Promotion of universal use of iodized and iron-fortified salt.

#### **4. Immunization**

- **Td Boosters:** Tetanus and adult-type Diphtheria (Td) vaccines at ages 10 and 16 years.
- **HPV Vaccine:** Now targeted at girls aged 9–14 years in school settings to prevent cervical cancer.

#### **5. Mental Health**

- Everything that happens in school affects the child's mental health.
- Teachers help identify "stormy" transitions during adolescence and refer children with emotional problems to child guidance clinics.

#### **6. Health Education**

- **Human Biology:** Teaching the structure and functions of the body.
- **Personal Hygiene:** Inculcating habits like handwashing, oral hygiene, and regular bathing.
- **Environment:** Safe drinking water and sanitation (Swachh Bharat Mission) in school premises.

---

### **III. Organization and Service Delivery**

The program operates through a decentralized, integrated mechanism.

|Level|Organization/Functionary|Key Role|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**National/State**|NHM & Ministry of Education|Policy formulation, funding, and target setting.|
|**District**|**District Early Intervention Centre (DEIC)**|Hub for referral, diagnosis, and management of screened cases.|
|**Implementation**|**RBSK Mobile Health Teams**|Two MOs, one Staff Nurse/ANM, and one Pharmacist who visit schools at least once a year.|
|**School Level**|**School Teacher**|First responder; performs initial eye screening and identifies deviations in behavior or health.|

---

### **IV. Brief Functions of the Programme**

1. **Conducting regular Wellness Check-ups** followed by advice and treatment.
2. **Provision of free referral and surgical services** for complex conditions (e.g., congenital heart defects) at tertiary centers.
3. **Active surveillance** for infectious conditions like tuberculosis and leprosy in children.
4. **Maintaining health records** to monitor the longitudinal progress of each child.

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points** ★

- **The 4Ds:** Defects, Deficiencies, Diseases, Developmental delays (RBSK).
- **School Screening Frequency:** At least **once a year** for children aged 6–18 years in Government/Government-aided schools.
- **Vision 2020 link:** School eye screening is a major target; the teacher is the "Vision Guardian" for the classroom.
- **Age group:** Birth to 18 years for RBSK; 10–19 years specifically for Adolescent health (ARSH/RKSK).

---

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: Why is the teacher vital for school health?**
    - **A:** They spend the most time with the child and can observe subtle changes in vision (squinting), hearing (not responding), or behavior (withdrawal) that periodic medical visits might miss.
- **Q: What is the "single-visit" approach in school eye care?**
    - **A:** Screening, diagnosis of refractive errors, and prescription of glasses are often coordinated to ensure the child receives help quickly.
- **Q: What vaccine is now specifically given to school-going girls in India?**
    - **A:** HPV vaccine (Human Papillomavirus) for cervical cancer prevention.

---

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Focusing only on Mid-day Meals:** Forgetting that school health includes comprehensive **RBSK screening** for 4Ds.
- **Ignoring the Age Range:** Confusing school health (6–18 yrs) with under-five care. School health includes **adolescents** up to 18 years.
- **Forgetting the Teacher's Role:** In exams, always highlight the **school teacher** as a critical functionary in the health system, especially for eye and mental health screening.
