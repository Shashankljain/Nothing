# National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP)

### **Introduction**

The National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP) is a centrally sponsored health scheme of the Government of India. Originally launched as the **National Leprosy Control Programme (NLCP) in 1955**, it was later renamed the **National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP) in 1983** after the introduction of Multi-Drug Therapy (MDT), which significantly accelerated the progress toward elimination.

★ **Important Exam Point:** India achieved the goal of **"Elimination of Leprosy as a public health problem"** (defined as a prevalence rate of less than 1 case per 10,000 population) at the national level in **December 2005**.

---

### **Aim and Vision**

The current vision of the programme, as outlined in the **Global Leprosy Strategy 2021–2030**, is **"Towards Zero Leprosy"**.

- **Vision:** Zero infection and disease, zero disability, and zero stigma and discrimination.
- **Medium-term Goal:** The elimination of leprosy, defined as the interruption of transmission.

---

### **Objectives**

The objectives have evolved from simple control to a roadmap for a "Leprosy Free India".

1. **Elimination:** Maintain and achieve a prevalence rate of less than 1 per 10,000 population in all districts of the country.
2. **Disability Reduction:** Strengthen disability prevention and medical rehabilitation (DPMR) of persons affected by leprosy.
3. **Stigma Reduction:** Reduce the level of stigma associated with the disease through intensive awareness.
4. **Early Detection:** Detect and treat new leprosy cases as early as possible to prevent Grade II disabilities.

---

### **Strategic Pillars (NSP 2023–2027)**

The current National Strategic Plan (2023–2027) is built on four core pillars:

- **Pillar 1:** Implement integrated, country-owned zero-leprosy roadmaps in all endemic countries.
- **Pillar 2:** Scale up leprosy prevention alongside integrated active case detection.
- **Pillar 3:** Manage leprosy and its complications, and prevent new disability.
- **Pillar 4:** Combat stigma and ensure human rights are respected.

---

### **Components of the Programme**

1. **Medical Measures:** Includes estimation of the problem, early case detection, and surveillance.
2. **Multi-Drug Therapy (MDT):** The backbone of the programme, provided free of cost to all patients.
3. **DPMR (Disability Prevention and Medical Rehabilitation):** Includes reaction management, reconstructive surgery (RCS), and providing Micro-cellular Rubber (MCR) footwear.
4. **Social Support:** Identifying and meeting the economic and social needs of patients and their families.
5. **IEC/BCC:** Information, Education, and Communication to generate demand for services and dispel myths.

---

### **Service Delivery Mechanism**

India has adopted a strategy of **decentralisation**, integrating leprosy services into the **general health care system**.

- **Primary Level:** PHCs, CHCs, and Sub-centres provide diagnosis and MDT delivery.
- **Secondary Level:** District Headquarter Hospitals and District Nucleus Units for management of complications and reactions.
- **Tertiary Level:** Central Government Institutes (e.g., CLTRI Chengalpattu), ICMR units (JALMA), and Medical Colleges for sophisticated care and reconstructive surgery.

---

### **Role of ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist)**

ASHAs play a critical "link worker" role at the village level to bring leprosy cases into the system.

- **Search/Detection:** Identifying suspected cases (ABSULS - ASHA Based Surveillance for Leprosy Suspects) before disability appears.
- **Follow-up:** Ensuring patients complete their treatment on time and monitoring for lepra reactions.
- **Self-care:** Advising disabled patients on proper care for their hands and feet.
- **Incentives:** ASHAs receive performance-based payments:
    - ₹250 for a confirmed diagnosis.
    - ₹400 for PB treatment completion and ₹600 for MB treatment completion.
    - ₹250 for early detection before visible deformity.

---

### **The Multi-Drug Therapy (MDT) Regimens**

MDT is essential to prevent drug resistance and interrupt transmission.

|**Type of Leprosy**|**Criteria**|**Drugs Used**|**Duration**|
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
|**Paucibacillary (PB)**|1–5 skin lesions; only 1 nerve involved; smear negative|Rifampicin, Dapsone, Clofazimine|6 blister packs within 9 months|
|**Multibacillary (MB)**|6 or more lesions; >1 nerve involved; smear positive|Rifampicin, Dapsone, Clofazimine|12 blister packs within 18 months|

_Note: India is implementing a revised regimen from 1st April 2025 where Clofazimine is added to the PB regimen as well._

---

### **Monitoring and Indicators**

The programme is evaluated using several key indicators to assess progress:

1. **Prevalence Rate (PR):** Number of cases on record per 10,000 population (Target: <1).
2. **Annual New Case Detection Rate (ANCDR):** New cases detected per 1,00,000 population (Target: <10).
3. **Treatment Completion Rate (TCR):** Used as a proxy for cure rate (Target: >90%).
4. **Grade II Disability Rate:** New cases with G2D per million population (Target: <1).

### **Recent Updates and Modifications**

- **Towards Zero Leprosy:** Shift in focus from mere "elimination" to ending transmission entirely.
- **LCDC (Leprosy Case Detection Campaign):** Intensive door-to-door search in high-prevalence districts.
- **Sparsh Leprosy Awareness:** Launched in 2017 to reduce stigma through Gram Sabhas.
- **Nikusht 2.0:** A web-based digital reporting and drug management system.
- **Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP):** Administering a single dose of Rifampicin (SDR) to eligible contacts of newly diagnosed patients to interrupt the chain of transmission.

---

### **★ High-Yield Exam Points**

- **Elimination Criteria:** Prevalence < 1 per 10,000 population.
- **Leprosy Milestone:** National elimination achieved in Dec 2005.
- **The "Cardinal Signs":** 1. Hypopigmented/reddish patch with sensory deficit; 2. Thickened peripheral nerves with weakness; 3. Demonstration of M. leprae in skin smear.
- **Secondary Prevention:** MDT is the primary tool for secondary prevention (halts progress and prevents complications).

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

**Q: What is a "Released From Treatment" (RFT) case?** **A:** A patient who has successfully completed the full course of MDT as prescribed (6 months for PB, 12 months for MB).

**Q: What is the difference between "Elimination" and "Eradication"?** **A:** Elimination is the reduction of disease to a very low level (regional/national), whereas eradication is the permanent termination of the infectious agent globally.

**Q: Name the drugs in the MDT "Blister Pack."** **A:** Rifampicin, Clofazimine, and Dapsone.

**Q: What is the significance of Grade II disability?** **A:** It represents visible deformity or severe visual impairment, signifying a delay in diagnosis and treatment.
