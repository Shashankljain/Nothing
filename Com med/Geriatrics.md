[[PM in geriatrics]]
**Geriatrics** is the branch of medicine that focuses on the health and care of the elderly. In the Indian context, individuals aged **60 years and above** are classified as the geriatric population. This group is growing rapidly due to the "demographic transition," where falling birth rates and increasing life expectancy have led to an aging population.

---

### **I. The Magnitude of the Problem**

- **The Gray Revolution:** India’s population aged 60 and older is projected to increase from 8% in 2010 to **19% by 2050**.
- **Disease Burden:** By 2030, nearly **45% of India's total disease burden** is projected to be borne by older adults.
- **Vulnerability of Women:** Aged women often face a "double burden" of social neglect and biological vulnerability, particularly following menopause, which accelerates conditions like osteoporosis and cardiovascular disease.

---

### **II. Common Chronic Health Problems (NCDs) in Aged Women** ★ Important Exam Point

The geriatric population in India suffers from a high prevalence of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs).

|**System/Category**|**Chronic Conditions**|**Significance in Women**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Cardiovascular**|Hypertension, Ischaemic Heart Disease (IHD).|Hypertension prevalence is **>60%** in those over 60. Post-menopausal women lose the protective effect of oestrogen.|
|**Metabolic**|Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus.|India has 77 million diabetics; prevalence increases sharply with age.|
|**Neurological**|Stroke, Alzheimer’s, and Dementia.|**68% of deaths** from Alzheimer's and other dementias globally occur in women.|
|**Musculoskeletal**|Osteoarthritis, Osteoporosis.|Women are more susceptible to osteoporosis-related fractures due to post-menopausal bone density loss.|
|**Sensory**|Cataract, Glaucoma, Macular Degeneration.|**82% of all visually impaired** people are aged 50 and older. Cataract is responsible for 66.2% of blindness in India.|
|**Malignancies**|Breast Cancer, Cervical Cancer.|These are the top two cancers in Indian women. Breast cancer is occurring a decade earlier in India than in the West.|
|**Respiratory**|COPD, Asthma.|Often related to past exposure to indoor air pollution from solid cooking fuels.|

---

### **III. Risk Factors for Chronic Illnesses**

1. **Biological:** Senescence (genetically programmed aging), decline in cell-mediated immunity, and hormonal changes (menopause).
2. **Behavioural:** Tobacco use (both smoking and smokeless), physical inactivity, and unhealthy diets (high fat/salt).
3. **Psychosocial Stress:** Social isolation, loss of a spouse (widowhood), and economic insecurity ("Material Abandonment").

---

### **IV. Measures Taken to Prevent and Control These Problems in India** ★ Important Exam Point

Prevention is categorized into three levels, focusing on health promotion and specialized care.

#### **1. Primary Prevention (Health Promotion)**

- **Nutritional Guidance:** Adoption of the ICMR/NIN **"My Plate for the Day for 2000 Kcal"** to ensure balanced nutrition and prevent obesity/deficiencies.
- **Physical Activity:** Encouraging at least **150–300 minutes** of moderate aerobic activity weekly to prevent heart disease and diabetes.
- **Tobacco and Alcohol Control:** Implementation of the **Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products Act (COTPA) 2003** to reduce cancers and respiratory diseases.

#### **2. Secondary Prevention (Early Detection & Treatment)**

- **Screening Programmes:**
    - **Cervical Cancer:** Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid (VIA) or Pap Smear for early detection.
    - **Breast Cancer:** Training women in **Breast Self-Examination (BSE)** and clinical palpation.
    - **Hypertension/Diabetes:** Opportunistic screening during regular health facility visits.
- **NPCDCS:** The National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases, and Stroke provides integrated care for these chronic conditions.

#### **3. Tertiary Prevention (Rehabilitation & Specialized Care)**

- **NPHCE:** The **National Programme for Health Care of the Elderly** provides dedicated geriatric wards at District Hospitals and clinics at CHCs/PHCs.
- **Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY):** Provides financial risk protection for secondary and tertiary hospitalisation.
    - **Recent Update:** A new **distinct health card** has been introduced specifically for senior citizens aged **70 years and above** to ensure universal coverage.
- **Vision 2020:** Targets the elimination of avoidable blindness through free cataract surgeries for the elderly.

#### **4. Legislative Measures**

- **Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act (2007):** Provides a legal framework to ensure children/relatives provide maintenance to the elderly.

### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- **Definition:** Geriatric age starts at **60 years** in India.
- **Dementia Statistic:** **68%** of global dementia deaths are in women.
- **Primary Blindness Cause:** Cataract (66.2%).
- **New Initiative:** Ayushman Bharat card for those **age 70 and above**.
- **Mnemonic for Warning Signals of poor mental health (Menninger):** **"A-W-A-R-E"** (**A**nxious, **W**orrying always, **A**fraid without cause, **R**outine disturbed, **E**asily loses temper).

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: Why is cataract the leading cause of blindness in the elderly?**
    - **A:** It is a degenerative condition of the lens that progresses with age, and many elderly lack access to affordable surgical services.
- **Q: What is "Social Pathology" in the elderly?**
    - **A:** It refers to health problems (like depression or neglect) that arise from social conditions such as poverty, isolation, and the breakdown of the joint family system.
- **Q: How does menopause affect a woman's cardiovascular risk?**
    - **A:** Menopause leads to a decline in oestrogen, which was previously protective; this results in a "late relative rise" in blood pressure and increased IHD risk.

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Forgetting the Gender Aspect:** Students often list general geriatric problems but fail to highlight that **dementia**and **osteoporosis** disproportionately affect women.
- **Ignoring the age of 70:** Students often quote 60 for all programs, but the new Ayushman Bharat initiative specifically targets those **70 and above**.
- **Missing the Vision Guardian:** Failing to mention that visual impairment in the elderly is largely **avoidable**(cataract/refractive errors).
