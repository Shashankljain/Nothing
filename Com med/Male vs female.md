[[Geriatrics]]
**II. Common Chronic Health Problems (NCDs)** ★ Important Exam Point

The elderly suffer from a high prevalence of chronic conditions, often requiring long-term supervision and care.

|**System**|**Chronic Condition**|**Key Features in Geriatrics**|
|---|---|---|
|**Cardiovascular**|**Hypertension**|Prevalence is **>60%** in those over 60 years. Leading cause of IHD and Stroke.|
|**Cardiovascular**|**Ischaemic Heart Disease (IHD)**|The biggest killer globally; risk increases with advancing age.|
|**Neurological**|**Stroke**|Average age of occurrence is 73 in developed countries, but "strokes in the young" (<40) are significant in India.|
|**Neurological**|**Dementia/Alzheimer's**|In 2021, dementia was the 7th leading cause of death.|
|**Metabolic**|**Diabetes Mellitus**|Type 2 diabetes is gradual in onset and common in the elderly.|
|**Musculoskeletal**|**Osteoarthritis/Osteoporosis**|Degenerative joint diseases and fractures due to falls are assuming greater proportions.|
|**Respiratory**|**COPD/Asthma**|Often linked to past smoking or indoor air pollution from solid fuels.|
|**Malignancies**|**Cancer**|Aging is a major risk factor due to cumulative exposure to carcinogens.|

**IV. Comparison: Elderly Males vs. Elderly Females** ★ Important Exam Point

|**Factor**|**Elderly Males**|**Elderly Females**|
|---|---|---|
|**Mortality**|Generally higher mortality in adulthood.|Live longer but with higher morbidity ("Gender Paradox").|
|**Neurological**|Lower rates of dementia than females.|**68% of deaths** from Alzheimer’s and dementia globally occur in women.|
|**CVD Risk**|Risk starts earlier in life.|**Post-menopausal changes** lead to a "late relative rise" in blood pressure and CVD risk once estrogen protection is lost.|
|**Nutritional**|Higher rates of obesity in urban males.|Higher burden of **undernutrition** and anaemia (45.8% in SEAR).|
|**Cancers**|Top: Lip/oral cavity, Lung, Oesophagus (tobacco-related).|Top: **Breast Cancer** and **Cervical Cancer**.|
|**Social Factors**|Often face loss of status/income upon retirement.|Highly vulnerable to **social isolation** and "material abandonment" following widowhood.|

**V. Healthcare Needs and Preventive Measures**

**1. Primary Prevention (Promotion & Specific Protection)**

- **Nutrition:** Adopting the ICMR-NIN **"My Plate for the Day"** (2000 Kcal) and reducing salt intake (<5g/day).
- **Physical Activity:** Engaging in 150–300 minutes of moderate aerobic activity weekly to prevent muscle wasting and falls.
- **Immunization:** Vaccination against Influenza and Pneumococcal pneumonia (for high-risk groups).

**2. Secondary Prevention (Early Detection)**

- **Screening:** Periodic check-ups for hypertension, diabetes, and cancers (VIA for cervix, BSE for breast).
- **Vision Care:** Identification of refractive errors and cataract under the **National Programme for Control of Blindness and Visual Impairment**.

**3. Tertiary Prevention (Rehabilitation)**

- **NPHCE:** The National Programme for Health Care of the Elderly provides specialized geriatric services.
- **Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY):** A **new distinct health card** has been introduced for senior citizens aged **70 years and above** to ensure universal health coverage.
- **Self-Care:** Teaching the elderly to monitor their own BP and blood sugar to reduce the burden on official services.
