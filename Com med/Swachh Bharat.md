[[Health programmes in India]]
#new 
**Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Clean India Mission)** is a major national health and sanitation initiative launched by the Government of India to address the country's critical challenges in environmental sanitation and hygiene.

---

### **I. Introduction**

- **Definition:** Swachh Bharat Abhiyan is a comprehensive national campaign designed to achieve universal sanitation coverage and eliminate the practice of open defecation.
- **Launch Year:** The programme was officially launched in **2015** (according to the significant events timeline in the sources).
- **Core Philosophy:** It is aligned with the principle of **"Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas"** (Together with all, Development for all), emphasizing that the first claim on development belongs to the poor and calling for security and dignity for all, especially women.

---

### **II. Aim and Objectives**

The primary aim is to transform India into a clean and healthy nation by improving environmental development with respect to water, air, and soil.

**Specific Objectives:** ★ Important Exam Point

1. **Access to Sanitation:** To ensure access to safe water and sanitation for all citizens.
2. **Ending Open Defecation:** To eliminate the age-old practice of open-field defecation, which is deeply rooted in traditional cultural behaviour but is a major health hazard.
3. **Sustainable Development:** To improve environmental development by treating climate change challenges as opportunities.
4. **National Health Policy Target:** The National Health Policy (2017) integrated the mission with the goal of providing access to safe water and sanitation to all by the year **2020**.

---

### **III. Public Health Significance**

The programme is vital because much of the ill-health in India is attributed to poor environmental sanitation.

1. **Prevention of Fecal-Oral Infections:** Improper excreta disposal is a primary cause of soil and water pollution. The mission directly combats "fecal-oral" infections acquired through contaminated food and drink.
2. **Disease Control:** By improving sanitation, the programme helps reduce the incidence of:
    - **Bacterial Diseases:** Typhoid and paratyphoid fevers, bacillary dysentery, and cholera.
    - **Viral Diseases:** Viral hepatitis, poliomyelitis, and rotavirus diarrhoea.
    - **Parasitic Infections:** Amoebiasis, giardiasis, and helminthic infestations like hookworm (associated with walking barefoot on polluted soil).
3. **Reducing Mortality:** Improving environmental sanitation is crucial for reducing high death rates and infant mortality rates, which are often driven by defective sanitation.

---

### **IV. Strategy: Overcoming Social Resistance**

The sources highlight that the problem of rural sanitation is not just a lack of facilities but a deep-seated resistance to their use.

- **Behavioral Change:** Many villagers traditionally believe latrines are associated with bad smells, are breeding places for flies, or are unnecessary where open fields are available.
- **Health Education:** The solution lies in teaching the community _why_ latrines are important using direct discussion, demonstrations, and visual aids.
- **Integrated Approach:** The mission encourages the construction of low-cost, water-seal sanitary latrines (like the **Sulabh Shauchalaya** model) which require very little water and convert excreta into manure through bacterial decomposition.

- **NHP 2017 Goal:** Access to safe water and sanitation for all by 2020.

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: Why is ending open defecation a priority in the Swachh Bharat Mission?**
    - **A:** Open defecation pollutes water and soil, promotes fly breeding, and facilitates the transmission of numerous enteric and helminthic diseases.
- **Q: What are the "Five Ps" of the new Sustainable Development agenda that includes sanitation?**
    - **A:** People, planet, prosperity, peace, and partnership.
- **Q: How does poor sanitation affect children specifically?**
    - **A:** Children are particularly exposed to infections when playing or bathing in surface water contaminated by overflowing septic tanks or poor drainage, leading to high morbidity and mortality.

---

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Confusing Launch Dates:** Stating the mission began in 2014 (the actual announcement date) vs 2015 (the year it became a major operational milestone in the sources' timeline).
- **Narrow Scope:** Focusing only on "latrines" and forgetting that the mission also encompasses **safe drinking water**and overall **environmental development**.
- **Ignoring Social Factors:** Forgetting to mention that the biggest obstacle to the mission is often cultural habits and the need for **behavioral change communication (BCC)**.
