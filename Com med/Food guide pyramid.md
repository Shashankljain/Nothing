[[NUTRITION]]
A **Food Guide Pyramid** is a visual tool used in nutrition education to help individuals choose a balanced diet by categorizing food into different groups based on their nutrient density and recommending portion sizes. In recent years, the **National Institute of Nutrition (NIN) and ICMR** have updated these guidelines to the **"My Plate for the Day"**concept to better reflect the nutritional needs of the Indian population.

---

### **I. Introduction and Definition**

- **Definition:** A food guide is a graphic representation of the types and proportions of foods that should be consumed daily to achieve a balanced diet.
- **Purpose:** To translate scientific nutrient requirements (RDA) into simple, everyday food choices for the general public.
- **Evolution:** While the traditional "pyramid" structure was used for decades, the current 2024 recommendations focus on the **"My Plate for the Day"** model, specifically designed for a **2000 Kcal** intake.

---

### **II. Core Components of the Food Guide** ★ Important Exam Point

The guide classifies "Nutrient Agents" into specific food groups to ensure all essential vitamins, minerals, and macronutrients are consumed.
![[image 7.jpg]]

1. **Cereals and Millets:** Provide the bulk of energy. The guide emphasizes whole grains over refined carbohydrates.
2. **Pulses, Legumes, and Nuts:** Primary sources of protein, especially in vegetarian diets.
3. **Milk and Milk Products:** Essential for calcium and high-quality protein.
4. **Fruits and Vegetables:** Sources of micronutrients (vitamins and minerals) and dietary fibre.
5. **Fats and Oils:** Concentrated energy sources; the guide recommends limiting saturated fats and replacing them with polyunsaturated fats.

---

### **III. The Modern Approach: "My Plate for the Day" (2024)** ★ Important Exam Point

The source material highlights a major shift in nutritional guidance as of **2024**.

- **Reference Calorie:** The current model is based on **2000 Kcal/day**, which is the average requirement for a healthy adult.
- **HFSS Guidelines:** A new and critical component is the recommendation to avoid or strictly limit **High Fat, Sugar, and Salt (HFSS)** foods.
- **Ultra-Processed Foods:** The 2024 guidelines strongly discourage the consumption of ultra-processed foods to prevent the rise of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) like obesity and diabetes.

---

### **IV. Why Do We Use a Food Guide?**

- **Preventing "Hidden Hunger":** Ensures adequate intake of micronutrients (Zinc, Iodine, Iron, Vitamin A) even if calorie intake is sufficient.
- **Combating the "Triple Burden":** Addresses the simultaneous presence of undernutrition (stunting/wasting), micronutrient deficiencies, and overnutrition (obesity) in the same community.
- **NCD Prevention:** Adhering to a prudent diet (low salt, high fibre, complex carbs) reduces the risk of Coronary Heart Disease (CHD), Hypertension, and Stroke.

---

### **V. Dietary Goals of the Guide** ★ Important Exam Point

|**Nutrient**|**Recommended Goal/Action**|
|:--|:--|
|**Total Fat**|**20–30%** of total energy.|
|**Saturated Fat**|**Less than 10%** of total energy.|
|**Salt (Sodium)**|**5 g or less** per day.|
|**Dietary Fibre**|Minimum **20 g** daily.|
|**Free Sugars**|**Less than 10%** (ideally <5%) of energy.|

### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- **2000 Kcal:** The standard calorie base for the new "My Plate" concept.
- **HFSS:** This stands for **H**igh **F**at, **S**ugar, and **S**alt—a keyword in any modern nutrition answer.
- **The 5g Rule:** Daily salt intake must not exceed 5 grams to prevent hypertension.
- **Protein Quality:** The hen's egg is the **Reference Protein** with a Net Protein Utilization (NPU) of **100**.

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: What is the main difference between the old Pyramid and the new 'My Plate'?**
    - **A:** 'My Plate' is more practical and emphasizes the proportion of food on an actual plate, whereas the pyramid focused on tiers of food groups. The 2024 update specifically highlights avoiding **HFSS** and **ultra-processed foods**.
- **Q: Why is salt limited to 5g in the food guide?**
    - **A:** High salt intake is a major determinant of raised blood pressure and increased cardiovascular risk.
- **Q: What is "Hidden Hunger"?**
    - **A:** It refers to micronutrient deficiencies (vitamins and minerals) that occur even when a person's calorie (macronutrient) intake is adequate.

---

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Using Outdated Statistics:** Students often quote old salt or fat limits. Always mention the **2024 ICMR-NIN recommendations**.
- **Ignoring Fibre:** Forgetting that **20g of dietary fibre** is a mandatory daily goal for preventing diabetes and obesity.
- **Narrow Focus:** Only discussing energy/calories. A food guide is equally about **micronutrient density** and preventing "Hidden Hunger".
