[[AMB]]
**Anemia Mukt Bharat (AMB)** is an intensified national strategy launched by the Government of India to accelerate the decline of anaemia prevalence across all age groups. It represents a shift from the previous **National Iron Plus Initiative (NIPI)** toward a more comprehensive, life-cycle approach to combat nutritional deficiencies and "hidden hunger".

---

### **I. Introduction and Definition**

- **Definition:** Anemia Mukt Bharat is a multi-sectoral strategy designed to reduce the prevalence of anaemia by **3 percentage points per year** among target groups.
- **Significance:** Anaemia remains a massive public health challenge in India, particularly among children and women of reproductive age. Globally, 45.8% of women in the WHO South-East Asia Region are anaemic; AMB serves as the primary vehicle to address this burden through the **National Health Mission (NHM)**.

---

### **II. The 6x6x6 Strategy** ★ Important Exam Point

The program is structured around the **"6x6x6" framework**, which targets 6 beneficiary groups, provides 6 interventions, and operates through 6 institutional mechanisms.

#### **1. Six Target Beneficiaries (The Life-Cycle Approach)**

AMB ensures age- and dose-specific supplementation from infancy to old age.

|**Group**|**Age/Category**|
|:--|:--|
|**1. Children**|6–59 months.|
|**2. Children**|5–9 years (school-going).|
|**3. Adolescents**|10–19 years (boys and girls).|
|**4. WRA**|Women of Reproductive Age (15–49 years; non-pregnant/non-lactating).|
|**5. Pregnant Women**|Throughout the gestational period.|
|**6. Lactating Mothers**|Postpartum support.|

#### **2. Six Key Interventions**

1. **Prophylactic IFA Supplementation:** Distribution of **Iron and Folic Acid (IFA)** tablets and syrups based on age-specific doses.
2. **Deworming:** Bi-annual mass deworming (Albendazole) to prevent blood loss from intestinal parasite infestations.
3. **BCC (Behaviour Change Communication):** Massive awareness campaigns to promote iron-rich diets and improve compliance.
4. **Testing and Treatment:** Using digital invasive or non-invasive haemoglobinometers for point-of-care testing and managing cases at health facilities.
5. **Food Fortification:** Mandating iron-fortified salt and other staples to combat "Hidden Hunger".
6. **Addressing Non-nutritional Causes:** Managing anaemia caused by malaria, fluorosis, and other endemic infections.

#### **3. Six Institutional Mechanisms**

1. **Intra-ministerial Coordination:** Collaboration between Health, WCD, and Education ministries.
2. **National Anemia Mukt Bharat Unit:** Dedicated monitoring cell.
3. **Supply Chain Management:** Ensuring an uninterrupted supply of IFA and deworming drugs.
4. **AMB Dashboard:** A web-based monitoring system for real-time data tracking.
5. **Convergence:** Linking with programs like **Poshan Abhiyaan** and **Swachh Bharat**.
6. **Research & Innovation:** Advancing diagnostic tools and treatment protocols.

### **III. Strategic Components of Service Delivery** ★ Important Exam Point

- **Prophylactic Doses (from NLEM 2022):**
    - **Adults/Pregnant Women:** Tablet containing **100 mg elemental iron** and **500 mcg Folic acid**.
    - **Children (6–59 months):** Oral liquid containing **20 mg elemental iron** and **100 mcg Folic acid** per ml.
    - **Adolescents (WIFS):** **Weekly Iron and Folic Acid Supplementation** containing **60 mg elemental iron** and **400 mcg Folic acid**.
- **Point of Contact:** Services are delivered via the **Anganwadi Centre (AWC)**, schools, and **Ayushman Arogya Mandirs** (Health and Wellness Centres).
- **Role of Health Workers:** **ASHAs (Accredited Social Health Activists)** and **ANMs (Auxiliary Nurse Midwives)**perform community mobilization and screening for the "4Ds" (Defects, Deficiencies, Diseases, and Developmental delays).

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points** ★

- **Full Form:** **IFA** = Iron and Folic Acid; **WIFS** = Weekly Iron-Folic Acid Supplementation.
- **Target:** AMB aims to reduce anaemia prevalence by **3% per year**.
- **Reference Calorie:** 2000 Kcal is the base for dietary recommendations to ensure micronutrient adequacy.
- **Mnemonic for 6x6x6:** Remember **"6 Groups, 6 Actions, 6 Enablers"**.
- **The "Plus" in NIPI:** Signifies the inclusion of all age groups, not just children and pregnant women.

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: What is the difference between AMB and the previous anaemia programs?**
    - **A:** AMB is more intensive, uses the 6x6x6 framework, includes boys/men in target groups, and addresses non-nutritional causes like malaria alongside iron deficiency.
- **Q: Why is Albendazole given under AMB?**
    - **A:** Soil-transmitted helminths (hookworms) cause chronic blood loss; deworming is essential for the iron supplementation to be effective.
- **Q: What is "Hidden Hunger"?**
    - **A:** It is a micronutrient deficiency where a person may consume enough calories but lacks vitamins and minerals like iron or iodine required for development.

---

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Ignoring non-nutritional causes:** Only writing about diet and forgetting that **Malaria** and **Hookworm** are major causes of anaemia in India.
- **Incorrect Dosing:** Confusing the pediatric oral liquid dose with the adult tablet dose. Always specify **elemental iron** content.
- **Confusing Ministries:** While the Ministry of Health (NHM) leads AMB, the Ministry of Women and Child Development (WCD) is the primary partner for AWC-based delivery.
