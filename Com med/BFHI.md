The **Baby Friendly Hospital Initiative (BFHI)** is a global programmatic effort launched by the **World Health Organization (WHO) and UNICEF** in 1991 [History]. It is designed to encourage maternity facilities to implement practices that protect, promote, and support breastfeeding, starting from the very first hour of a child's life [History].
[[PM in Obstetrics]]
### **Introduction and Definition**

- **Definition:** BFHI is a package of policies and clinical protocols that ensure infants get the best start in life through optimal feeding practices [History].
- **The "Baby Friendly" Status:** A hospital is designated as "Baby Friendly" when it has successfully implemented the **"Ten Steps to Successful Breastfeeding"** and complies with the _International Code of Marketing of Breast-milk Substitutes_ [History].

---

### **BFHI as a Global Health Indicator**

According to the **Global Reference List of Core Health Indicators (2018)**, BFHI status is a critical metric for monitoring health systems:

- **Indicator Category:** Service Coverage Indicator.
- **Sub-Category:** Reproductive, maternal, newborn, child, and adolescent health.
- **Specific Metric:** It tracks the proportion of institutional deliveries that occur specifically in **"baby-friendly" institutions**.

---

### **The "Ten Steps to Successful Breastfeeding"**

These ten steps are the "Gold Standard" for maternity care and are frequently asked in university examinations [History].
### PCA Immediate SER DND

|**Step**|**Category**|**Key Action Required**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Step 1**|**Policy**|Maintain a written infant feeding policy and comply with the International Code of Marketing [History].|
|**Step 2**|**Competency**|Ensure all healthcare staff have the knowledge and skills to support breastfeeding [History].|
|**Step 3**|**Antenatal Care**|Counsel all pregnant women about the benefits and management of breastfeeding [History].|
|**Step 4**|**Immediate Initiation**|Help mothers initiate breastfeeding **within 30 minutes** of birth (★ Important Exam Point) [History].|
|**Step 5**|**Support**|Show mothers how to breastfeed and maintain lactation even if separated from their infants [History].|
|**Step 6**|**Exclusive Feeding**|Give newborns **no food or drink other than breast milk**, unless medically indicated [History].|
|**Step 7**|**Rooming-in**|Allow mothers and infants to remain together **24 hours a day** [History].|
|**Step 8**|**Demand Feeding**|Encourage breastfeeding whenever the baby shows hunger cues [History].|
|**Step 9**|**No Artificial Teats**|Give no artificial teats or pacifiers (dummies) to breastfeeding infants [History].|
|**Step 10**|**Discharge Support**|Coordinate discharge so parents have access to community breastfeeding support groups [History].|

### **Public Health Significance**

1. **The "First Immunization":** Colostrum (the first milk produced in the first 3 days) is rich in antibodies and serves as the baby's first natural vaccine.
2. **Child Survival:** BFHI is a core component of the **RMNCH+A** strategy (Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent Health) to reduce the Neonatal Mortality Rate (NMR) [History].
3. **Nutrition Transition:** BFHI helps combat the "triple burden of malnutrition" by ensuring infants are not started on highly processed, energy-dense formula too early.

---

### **★ High-Yield Exam Points**

- **The 30-Minute Rule:** Breastfeeding should be initiated within 30 minutes of a normal delivery [History].
- **Rooming-in:** This practice facilitates bonding and demand feeding while reducing the risk of hospital-acquired infections [History].
- **Pre-lacteal feeds:** BFHI strictly prohibits giving "ghutti," honey, or glucose water (pre-lacteal feeds) as they interfere with breastfeeding and increase infection risks [History].

---

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

**Q: What is "Rooming-in"?** **A:** It is the practice where the mother and newborn stay in the same room 24 hours a day from birth to discharge [History].

**Q: Why are pacifiers and bottles discouraged in BFHI?** **A:** They cause "nipple confusion," making it difficult for the baby to latch onto the breast properly, and they are major sources of diarrhoeal infections [History].

**Q: Who are the founders of BFHI?** **A:** WHO and UNICEF [History].

### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **Launched:** 1991 by WHO/UNICEF.
- **Core Tool:** The "Ten Steps to Successful Breastfeeding."
- **Goal:** Protect and promote **Exclusive Breastfeeding (EBF)** for the first 6 months.
- **Key Indicator:** Used globally to measure the quality of maternal and newborn service coverage.
