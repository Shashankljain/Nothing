 **Navajaat Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (NSSK)** is a centrally sponsored program launched by the Government of India to address neonatal mortality by training healthcare providers in basic newborn care and resuscitation,. It focuses on the most critical period for a child's survival—the first few minutes and days of life.

---

### **I. Introduction**

- **Definition:** NSSK is a training-based initiative designed to ensure that every newborn receives a set of essential interventions at birth to prevent common causes of death such as birth asphyxia, hypothermia, and infections,.
- **Aim:** To reduce the Neonatal Mortality Rate (NMR) by strengthening the skills of health personnel (doctors, nurses, ANMs) who attend deliveries at various levels of the health system.
- **Public Health Significance:** Neonatal deaths (deaths within the first 28 days of life) contribute significantly to the Under-5 mortality rate in India. NSSK targets the "Golden Minute"—the first minute after birth—when the initiation of breathing is crucial.

---

### **II. Core Services under NSSK** ★ Important Exam Point

The program is built around two major components: **Essential Newborn Care (ENC)** and **Newborn Resuscitation**.

#### **1. Essential Newborn Care (ENC)**

These services are provided to every newborn regardless of their health status:

- **Prevention of Hypothermia (Warmth):** Ensuring the "Warm Chain" is maintained. This includes immediate drying of the baby and skin-to-skin contact with the mother.
- **Prevention of Infection (Aseptic Care):** Focuses on the "Five Cleans" for delivery (clean hands, clean surface, clean cord tie, clean blade, and clean cord care).
- **Early Initiation of Breastfeeding:** Encouraging the mother to start breastfeeding within one hour of birth to provide colostrum and essential nutrients.
- **Basic Care of the Cord, Skin, and Eyes:** Avoiding harmful traditional practices and ensuring clean handling.

#### **2. Newborn Resuscitation (Basic)**

This service is provided to babies who do not breathe or cry immediately after birth:

- **Identification of Birth Asphyxia:** Recognizing the baby’s inability to initiate or sustain spontaneous breathing at birth.
- **The "Golden Minute" Intervention:** Providing basic resuscitation steps (stimulation and bag-and-mask ventilation) within the first minute of life to prevent brain damage or death.
- **Clearance of Airway:** Ensuring the airway is open if the baby has difficulty breathing.

---

### **III. Integration with Other Programs**

NSSK works in tandem with other national initiatives to ensure comprehensive care:

- **Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK):** While NSSK provides the _skills/training_, JSSK (launched in 2011) provides the _entitlements_. Under JSSK, sick newborns are entitled to free drugs, free diagnostics, free diet, and free transport from home to the facility.
- **IMNCI (Integrated Management of Neonatal and Childhood Illness):** NSSK provides the initial "at birth" care, which is then followed by the IMNCI protocols for the management of the newborn up to 28 days and beyond.
- **Facility-Based Newborn Care:** Serious cases identified during NSSK are referred to **Special Newborn Care Units (SNCU)** or **Newborn Stabilization Units (NBSU)**.

---

### **IV. Strategy for Implementation**

- **Standardized Training:** A 2-day training module for healthcare providers focusing on hands-on practice using mannequins.
- **Provider Base:** Target trainees include Medical Officers, Staff Nurses, and Auxiliary Nurse Midwives (ANMs) working at PHCs, CHCs, and District Hospitals.
- **Monitoring:** Regular supervision to ensure that the skills learned are applied during every delivery.

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points** ★

- **NSSK Focus:** Basic Newborn Care and Resuscitation.
- **The "Golden Minute":** The first minute after birth is the primary window for resuscitation.
- **ENC Components:** Warmth, Cleanliness (Infection control), and Breastfeeding.
- **JSSK vs. NSSK:** NSSK is about training/skills; JSSK is about free services/entitlements.

---

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: What is the most important intervention for a baby who doesn't cry at birth?**
    - **A:** Basic Newborn Resuscitation within the "Golden Minute" using bag-and-mask ventilation.
- **Q: How is hypothermia prevented immediately after birth?**
    - **A:** Immediate drying of the baby and placing them in skin-to-skin contact with the mother (Kangaroo Mother Care).
- **Q: What are the "Five Cleans"?**
    - **A:** Clean hands, clean delivery surface, clean cord tie, clean blade for cutting the cord, and no application on the cord stump.

---

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Confusing JSSK and NSSK:** Students often list the free transport/diet (JSSK) as services of NSSK. Remember, NSSK is strictly about **Basic Newborn Care and Resuscitation**.
- **Forgetting the "Golden Minute":** This is a key phrase examiners look for when discussing neonatal resuscitation.
- **Ignoring Breastfeeding:** Students often think NSSK is only for "sick" babies; however, promoting early breastfeeding is a core service for _all_ newborns under NSSK.
