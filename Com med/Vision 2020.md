[[Blindness]]
**Vision 2020: The Right to Sight** is a global initiative launched by the World Health Organization (WHO) and a coalition of international agencies to eliminate avoidable blindness by the year 2020. It represents a paradigm shift in eyecare by focusing on "Rights issues," recognizing sight as a fundamental human right.

---

### **I. Introduction and Definitions**

- **Definition:** Vision 2020 is a global initiative designed to eliminate avoidable blindness—defined as blindness that is either preventable or treatable—by the year 2020.
- **Launch Date:** It was launched by WHO on **18th February 1999**.
- **India's Adoption:** India adopted this global initiative in **2004**.
- **Core Philosophy:** The initiative moves away from a purely medical model to one centered on human rights, aiming to develop sustainable health systems.

---

### **II. Aim and Objectives**

The ultimate goal is to reduce the global burden of blindness so that it no longer constitutes a major public health problem.

**Specific Objectives:**

1. To eliminate avoidable blindness from major causes.
2. To assist member countries in developing sustainable eyecare systems.
3. To foster human resource development and infrastructure at all levels of the health system.

---

### **III. Target Diseases under Vision 2020**

The initiative prioritizes specific diseases that are the leading causes of avoidable blindness:

- **Cataract:** The leading cause of blindness in India (66.2%).
- **Refractive Errors and Low Vision:** A major cause of visual impairment, especially in children.
- **Childhood Blindness:** Including xerophthalmia (Vitamin A deficiency) and retinopathy of prematurity.
- **Glaucoma:** A growing cause of irreversible blindness.
- **Diabetic Retinopathy:** An emerging challenge due to the rising prevalence of diabetes.
- **Corneal Blindness:** Including trachoma and corneal ulcers.

---

### **IV. Strategy: The 4-Tier Infrastructure**

To achieve its goals, Vision 2020 proposed a structured, decentralized system of service delivery:

|Tier|Level of Care|Number (Target)|Key Functions|
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
|**Tier 1**|**Centres of Excellence**|20|National level; professional leadership, strategy development, and advanced research.|
|**Tier 2**|**Training Centres**|200|Tertiary level; provide sophisticated care (e.g., corneal grafting) and training for personnel.|
|**Tier 3**|**Service Centres**|2,000|Secondary level (District/Sub-district); focused on cataract surgery and other common eye surgeries.|
|**Tier 4**|**Vision Centres**|20,000|Primary level; provide refractive services, school screening, and basic eyecare.|

---

### **V. Human Resource Development**

- **Vision Guardian:** Under the Vision 2020 strategy, it is proposed to provide one "Vision Guardian" for every **5,000 population** to ensure community-level monitoring and referral.
- **Training:** Emphasis is placed on training ophthalmic surgeons, ophthalmic assistants, and school teachers for initial screening.

---

### **VI. Implementation in India: NPCBVI**

India has integrated the Vision 2020 goals into its **National Programme for Control of Blindness and Visual Impairment (NPCBVI)**.

**Major Activities:**

1. **Cataract Operations:** Aiming for 66 lakh operations per year.
2. **School Eye Screening:** Screening children aged 10–14 years for refractive errors.
3. **Vitamin A Prophylaxis:** Administering 200,000 IU doses every 6 months to children aged 1–6 years.
4. **Eye Donation:** Promotion of eye banking for corneal transplantation.
5. **SAFE Strategy for Trachoma:** (S)urgery, (A)ntibiotics, (F)acial cleanliness, and (E)nvironmental improvement.

### **High-Yield Exam Points** ★

- **Launch Year:** 1999 (Global) / 2004 (India).
- **The 4-Tier Model:** Know the numbers (20, 200, 2000, 20,000).
- **Leading Cause:** Cataract remains the principal focus.
- **India’s New Definition of Blindness (2017):** Visual acuity < 3/60 or inability to count fingers at 3 metres (brought in line with WHO criteria).

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: What is avoidable blindness?**
    - **A:** Blindness that can be either prevented (e.g., trachoma, xerophthalmia) or treated (e.g., cataract, refractive errors).
- **Q: What is the "Vision Guardian" concept?**
    - **A:** A community-level volunteer serving 5,000 people to identify and refer those with vision problems.
- **Q: Which cause of blindness was successfully eliminated in India?**
    - **A:** WHO declared India free from infective **trachoma** on 8th October 2024.

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Confusing Dates:** Mixing up the launch of NPCB (1976) with Vision 2020 (1999).
- **Obsolete Definitions:** Writing the old 6-metre definition of blindness instead of the current **3-metre** standard.
- **Infrastructure Errors:** Swapping the roles of Vision Centres (Primary) and Service Centres (Secondary). Remember: Vision Centres are the most numerous (20,000) and are at the grass-roots level.
