**Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)** is a significant health initiative launched under the National Health Mission (NHM) to provide comprehensive healthcare for children. It focuses on early identification and early intervention for a wide range of health conditions.

---

### **I. Introduction**

- **Definition:** RBSK is a child health screening and early intervention service program designed to improve the overall quality of life and survival of children by screening for "4Ds": **D**efects at birth, **D**eficiencies, **D**iseases, and **D**evelopmental delays including disability.
- **Target Beneficiaries:** It covers children from birth up to **18 years** of age.

---

### **II. Aim and Objectives**

The primary aim is to detect health conditions early to prevent progression into more serious disabilities or morbidities.

- **Early Detection:** To identify health problems at the earliest possible stage.
- **Free Treatment:** To provide free follow-up and management, including surgeries at tertiary levels, for identified conditions.
- **Surveillance Integration:** To serve as a platform for regular surveillance of infectious and chronic conditions, such as leprosy.

---

### **III. Service Delivery Mechanism** ★ Important Exam Point

The program utilizes a structured screening approach at different levels of the community and health system:

1. **For Preschool Children (0–6 years):**
    
    - Screening is conducted at **Anganwadi Centres** twice a year.
    - Focus is on early childhood development and birth defects.
2. **For School-age Children (6–18 years):**
    
    - Screening is conducted at **Government and Government-aided schools** at least once a year.
    - This group includes children in primary, secondary, and higher secondary sections.
3. **Mobile Health Teams:**
    
    - Dedicated teams consisting of Medical Officers (Ayush/MBBS), Staff Nurses/ANMs, and Pharmacists visit these institutions to perform the screenings.

---

### **IV. Components of Screening (The 4 Ds)**

While the program covers 30+ specific health conditions, they are categorized into four broad groups:

- **Defects at Birth:** e.g., Neural tube defects, Down's syndrome, Congenital heart disease.
- **Deficiencies:** e.g., Anaemia, Vitamin A deficiency, Malnutrition.
- **Diseases:** e.g., Skin conditions, Respiratory infections, and notably, **Leprosy** (RBSK is integrated with NLEP for active case detection in children).
- **Developmental Delays:** e.g., Vision or hearing impairment, neuromotor impairment, and learning disabilities.

---

### **V. Integration with Other National Programmes**

RBSK acts as a bridge between various health initiatives to ensure no child is "lost" to the system:

- **National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP):** RBSK is integrated into the operational strategy for active case detection and regular surveillance in children (0–18 years) to ensure detection at an early stage and prevent Grade II disabilities.
- **Tertiary Care:** Children identified with complex conditions (like congenital heart defects) are referred to District Early Intervention Centres (DEIC) or higher specialized hospitals for free surgical or medical management.

---

### **VI. Public Health Significance**

- **Reducing Mortality:** By treating deficiencies and infectious diseases early, it lowers the under-five and adolescent mortality rates.
- **Disability Prevention:** Early intervention for developmental delays significantly reduces the long-term impact of physical and mental handicaps.
- **Quality of Life:** It ensures that children can participate effectively in education and lead socially and economically productive lives.

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points** ★

- **Age Coverage:** Birth to 18 years.
- **The 4 Ds:** Defects at birth, Deficiencies, Diseases, Developmental delays.
- **Screening Sites:** Anganwadi Centres and Government schools.
- **Leprosy Link:** RBSK is a key strategy for the early detection of leprosy in the paediatric age group.

---

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: Where does the screening for RBSK take place for a 10-year-old?**
    - **A:** At their Government or Government-aided school.
- **Q: What are the "4Ds" in RBSK?**
    - **A:** Defects at birth, Deficiencies, Diseases, and Developmental delays.
- **Q: Who comprises the RBSK Mobile Health Team?**
    - **A:** Two Medical Officers, one Staff Nurse/ANM, and one Pharmacist.

---

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Incorrect Age Group:** Often limiting the program to "under-fives" or school children only; it covers the entire range from **0 to 18 years**.
- **Forgetting Integration:** Not mentioning that RBSK is used for specific disease surveillance like Leprosy or Tuberculosis.
- **Confusing with JSSK:** JSSK is about free entitlements for pregnant women and sick newborns; RBSK is specifically about the **systematic screening of all children** for the 4Ds.
