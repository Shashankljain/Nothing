[[Health programmes in India]]


### **National Rural Health Mission (NRHM)**

The **National Rural Health Mission (NRHM)** was launched on **5th April 2005** by the Government of India to provide accessible, affordable, and quality healthcare to the rural population, especially vulnerable groups like women and children. It is now a sub-mission under the **National Health Mission (NHM)**.

---

### **Objectives of NRHM (Quantitative Targets)**

The mission is guided by the quantitative goals set in the National Health Policy (NHP) 2017 and the NITI Aayog Three-Year Action Agenda:

- **Fertility:** Reduce Total Fertility Rate (TFR) to **2.1** at national and sub-national levels by 2025.
- **Maternal Mortality:** Reduce Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR) to **100 per lakh** live births by 2020.
- **Infant & Child Mortality:**
    - Reduce Infant Mortality Rate (IMR) to **28** by 2019.
    - Reduce Under-Five Mortality Rate (U5MR) to **23** by 2025.
- **Disease Control & Elimination:**
    - Eliminate **Leprosy** (status maintained), **Kala-azar** (by 2017), and **Lymphatic Filariasis** in endemic pockets.
    - Achieve and maintain a cure rate of **>85%** in new sputum-positive TB cases and reach elimination by 2025.
    - Reduce premature mortality from **NCDs** (Non-Communicable Diseases) by **25%** by 2025.
- **Blindness:** Reduce prevalence to **0.25/1000** and the disease burden by one-third by 2025.

---

### **Core Strategies of NRHM**

NRHM employs several structural and operational strategies to bridge gaps in rural healthcare:

1. **Community Participation:** Engaging the community through the **Accredited Social Health Activist (ASHA)** and Village Health, Sanitation and Nutrition Committees (VHSNC).
2. **Infrastructure Strengthening:** Ensuring health facilities (Sub-centres, PHCs, CHCs) meet the **Indian Public Health Standards (IPHS)**.
3. **Flexible Financing:** Establishing **Rogi Kalyan Samitis (RKS)** (Hospital Management Societies) to allow local health institutions to manage funds and improve accountability.
4. **Mainstreaming AYUSH:** Integrating Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy into the formal public health system.
5. **Decentralized Planning:** Moving toward a "bottom-up approach" where targets are set based on the specific demands of the community.
6. **Inter-sectoral Coordination:** Coordinating with departments of water, sanitation, and nutrition (e.g., Swachh Bharat Mission).

### **Accredited Social Health Activist (ASHA)**

The ASHA is the key "link worker" between the rural community and the health system.

#### **1. Selection Criteria & Norms ★**

- **Population Norm:** 1 ASHA per 1,000 population (in tribal/hilly areas, 1 per village/habitation).
- **Gender:** Must be a woman.
- **Status:** A permanent resident of the village (preferably Married/Divorced/Widow).
- **Age:** 25–45 years.
- **Education:** Literate, with formal education up to **Class 10**.

#### **2. Job Responsibilities of ASHA**

ASHA workers act as health educators, providers, and mobilizers:

- **Community Mobilization:** Facilitates access to health services like immunization, ANC, and ICDS.
- **Counseling:** Educates mothers on birth preparedness, safe delivery, breastfeeding, and contraception.
- **Escort Services:** Accompanies pregnant women to health facilities for institutional delivery (supported by **Janani Suraksha Yojana**).
- **Medical Care:** Provides first aid and primary care for minor ailments (e.g., diarrhea, fever).
- **Depot Holder:** Stocks essential supplies like **ORS**, **IFA tablets**, Oral Contraceptive Pills (**OCP**), and condoms.
- **Informer:** Reports births, deaths, and disease outbreaks to the health system.
- **DOTS Provider:** Acts as a provider for Tuberculosis treatment under the National TB Elimination Programme.

---

### **★ Mnemonic for ASHA Job Responsibilities ★**

To remember the primary roles of an ASHA, use the word: **"M.E.D.I.C.A.L."**

- **M** – **M**obilizer (Facilitates community access to health services).
- **E** – **E**scort (Accompanies women for institutional delivery).
- **D** – **D**epot Holder (Stocks ORS, OCPs, Condoms, IFA).
- **I** – **I**nformer (Notifies system of births, deaths, and outbreaks).
- **C** – **C**ounselor (Advises on nutrition, sanitation, and MCH).
- **A** – **A**gent for National Programs (DOTS agent for TB, Malaria surveillance).
- **L** – **L**ink worker (The bridge between community and health centres).

### **ASHA's Post-Natal Home Visit Schedule ★**

A crucial exam point is the number of mandatory visits ASHA makes after delivery:

- **Home Delivery:** 7 visits (Days 1, 3, 7, 14, 21, 28, 42).
- **Institutional Delivery:** 6 visits (Starts from Day 3, as Day 1 is in the facility).
- **LSCS (C-Section):** 5 visits (Days 1 and 3 are skipped).

### **High-Yield Exam Points ★**

- ★ **NRHM Launch:** 2005.
- ★ **ASHA Selection:** Woman, resident, 25-45 years, 10th pass, 1 per 1000 population.
- ★ **ASHA TB Role:** She is a **DOTS Agent** and receives a financial incentive for every patient completing treatment.
- ★ **Rogi Kalyan Samiti:** A strategy for hospital management and inter-sectoral coordination.
- ★ **JSY:** Janani Suraksha Yojana is the conditional cash transfer scheme ASHA uses to promote institutional delivery.

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

1. **Q: Who trains the ASHA worker?**
    - **A:** The **ANM** (Auxiliary Nurse Midwife) at the sub-centre level provides training and guidance to ASHA.
2. **Q: Is ASHA a government employee?**
    - **A:** No, she is a community health volunteer paid via performance-based **incentives**.
3. **Q: What is the "Depot Holder" role?**
    - **A:** She maintains a stock of essential items like ORS, pills, and condoms to provide them at the doorstep of villagers.
4. **Q: How many visits for a home delivery?**
    - **A:** 7 visits within 42 days.
