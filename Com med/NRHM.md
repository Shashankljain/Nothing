[[Health programmes in India]]
 # National Rural Health Mission (NRHM)

### **1. Introduction**

The **National Rural Health Mission (NRHM)** was launched on **12th April 2005** by the Government of India. It was designed to address the health needs of the rural population, particularly in states with weak health infrastructure and poor health indicators.

In **2013**, the NRHM was subsumed under the **National Health Mission (NHM)**, which now includes both the Rural (NRHM) and Urban (NUHM) components.

★ **Important Exam Point:** NRHM was established to provide **accessible, affordable, and quality health care** to the rural population, especially vulnerable groups like women and children.

---

### **2. Objectives of NRHM**

The primary goal of the mission is to improve the health status of the rural population through various quantitative and qualitative targets.

- **Reduction of Mortality:** Aimed at significantly reducing the **Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR)** and **Infant Mortality Rate (IMR)**.
- **Universal Access:** Providing universal access to public health services such as Women’s and Children's health, nutrition, and sanitation.
- **Disease Control:** Prevention and control of both communicable (e.g., Malaria, TB, HIV) and non-communicable diseases.
- **Population Stabilization:** Achieving a Total Fertility Rate (TFR) of 2.1 to stabilize population growth.
- **Mainstreaming AYUSH:** Integrating indigenous systems of medicine (Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy) into the mainstream health delivery system.

---

### **3. Key Strategies of NRHM**

To achieve its objectives, NRHM employs several innovative strategies:

- **ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist):** The creation of a cadre of trained female community health activists to act as a link between the community and the health system.
- **Strengthening Infrastructure:** Upgrading Primary Health Centres (PHCs) and Community Health Centres (CHCs) to provide **24/7 services**.
- **Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY):** A conditional cash transfer scheme to incentivize institutional deliveries and reduce maternal/neonatal deaths.
- **Village Health and Nutrition Days (VHND):** Organizing monthly outreach sessions at the village level for immunization and health check-ups.
- **Integration of Programs:** Bringing vertical health programs (like the National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme) under one common umbrella.
- **Rogi Kalyan Samitis (RKS):** Establishing hospital management committees to ensure community participation and accountability in hospital functioning .

---

### **4. Job Responsibilities of ASHA**

The ASHA is the "Heart" of the NRHM at the village level. She is a trained female resident of the village (usually 1 per 1000 population) .

#### **A. Primary Roles**

1. **Health Educator:** She provides information to the community about primary health practices (nutrition, hygiene, pregnancy care) .
2. **Community Mobilizer:** She motivates mothers to access health services like Antenatal Care (ANC), Institutional Delivery, and Full Immunization.
3. **Depot Holder:** She maintains a stock of essential items like **Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS)**, Iron & Folic Acid (IFA) tablets, oral contraceptives, and condoms .
4. **Escort/Companion:** She accompanies pregnant women and sick children to the nearest health facility (PHC/CHC) for treatment or delivery .

#### **B. Clinical and Support Tasks**

- **Home-Based Newborn Care (HBNC):** Conducting a series of home visits (7 for home delivery, 6 for institutional) to monitor the health of the newborn and mother .
- **Disease Surveillance:** Tracking vital events (births and deaths) and reporting disease outbreaks in her village .
- **DOTS Provider:** Acting as a community-level provider for Tuberculosis treatment .
- **First Aid:** Providing basic medical care for minor ailments like diarrhoea and fevers .

#### **C. Coordination Roles**

- **With Anganwadi Worker (AWW):** Helps organize the monthly Village Health and Nutrition Day (VHND) .
- **With ANM:** Assists the Auxiliary Nurse Midwife in identifying "left-out" children for immunization and educating mothers on danger signs .

---

### **★ High-Yield Exam Points**

- **Selection Criteria for ASHA:** Must be a woman (25–45 years), permanent resident of the village, and educated preferably up to the **10th class** .
- **The "5 Cleans":** Under NRHM, ASHAs promote clean delivery practices: Clean hands, surface, cord cut, cord tie, and cord stump.
- **Population Norm:** 1 ASHA per 1,000 population .
- **Financial Incentive:** ASHA is not a salaried employee but receives performance-based incentives (e.g., ₹600 for facilitating a rural institutional delivery under JSY) .

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

**Q: When was NRHM launched?** **A:** 12th April 2005.

**Q: Who is a "Link Worker"?** **A:** The ASHA is referred to as a link worker because she connects the rural community to the formal public health system .

**Q: What are the main indicators of NRHM success?** **A:** Significant reduction in IMR and MMR, and increase in institutional delivery rates.

### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **Mission:** National Rural Health Mission (2005) $\rightarrow$ Now National Health Mission (2013).
- **Focus:** Rural health, IMR/MMR reduction, 24/7 PHCs.
- **Key Pillars:** ASHA, JSY, VHND, and Rogi Kalyan Samiti.
- **ASHA Role:** Educate, Mobilize, Depot holder, and Escort.
- **Incentive Scheme:** Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) is the major driver for institutional delivery .
