# **Integrated Counselling and Testing Centre (ICTC)**

### **1. Introduction and Definition**

An **Integrated Counselling and Testing Centre (ICTC)** is a health facility where a person is counselled and tested for HIV, either on their own initiative (Voluntary) or as advised by a health provider. It is a critical component of the **National AIDS Control Programme (NACP)** and serves as the **"Gateway"** to the entire spectrum of HIV/AIDS prevention, treatment, care, and support services.

★ **Important Exam Point:** The integration of **professional counselling** with medical testing is what distinguishes an ICTC from a regular diagnostic laboratory.

---

### **2. The WHO "5Cs" Principles**

According to the sources, every ICTC must strictly adhere to five core principles to ensure ethical standards and high-quality care:

1. **Consent:** Testing must be voluntary and based on informed consent; compulsory testing is discouraged.
2. **Confidentiality:** The HIV status of the client and their records must be kept private.
3. **Counselling:** Includes both pre-test (risk assessment) and post-test (results delivery and psychological support) counselling.
4. **Correct Test Results:** Ensuring accurate results through quality-assured testing protocols.
5. **Connection:** Direct and rapid linkage to care, support, and treatment services, specifically ART centres.

---

### **3. Objectives and Functions**

- **Early Detection:** Identifying HIV-positive individuals as early as possible to improve health outcomes and reduce transmission.
- **Prevention of Vertical Transmission:** Providing testing and counselling for pregnant women to prevent parent-to-child transmission (now integrated into the EVTH—Elimination of Vertical Transmission of HIV—framework).
- **Linkage to "Treat All":** Facilitating the immediate initiation of **Antiretroviral Therapy (ART)** for anyone who tests positive, regardless of their CD4 count or clinical stage.
- **Behaviour Change:** Educating clients on risk reduction, safe sexual practices, and condom promotion.
- **Surveillance:** Data from ICTCs helps in monitoring the magnitude and trends of the HIV epidemic in various populations.

---

### **4. The Diagnostic Process at ICTC**

In India, the diagnostic algorithm at an ICTC is designed to be highly accurate:

- **Serial Testing:** HIV status is confirmed using **three different rapid test kits** [Previous Turns].
- **Test 1 (Screening):** Usually a high-sensitivity test to ensure no positive case is missed.
- **Tests 2 & 3 (Confirmatory):** These have higher specificity to rule out false positives.
- **Confirmation:** A person is declared **HIV-positive** only if all three different tests are reactive (positive).

---

### **5. Management After Diagnosis**

Once a client is confirmed HIV-positive, the ICTC ensures they are transitioned into the care system:

1. **Post-test Counselling:** Disclosure of results with psychological support.
2. **Baseline Investigations:** Includes screening for co-infections like **Tuberculosis**.
3. **Linkage:** Direct referral to an ART centre for lifelong treatment initiation.

---

### **★ High-Yield Exam Points**

- **The Window Period:** Counselors must explain that a negative result might occur if the person is in the "Window Period" (typically 2–12 weeks post-exposure), requiring a re-test after 3 months [Previous Turns].
- **95-95-95 Strategy:** The first "95" of the UNAIDS targets refers to the goal that 95% of all people living with HIV should **know their status** through testing at facilities like ICTCs [Previous Turns].
- **Provider-Initiated Testing (PITC):** While voluntary testing is encouraged, health providers are trained to initiate testing for patients with symptoms suggestive of HIV or those with TB [Previous Turns].

---

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

**Q: What is the full form of ICTC?** **A:** Integrated Counselling and Testing Centre.

**Q: Can HIV testing be made mandatory for a job?** **A:** No. Testing must always be voluntary and based on informed consent according to the "5Cs".

**Q: Where are ICTCs usually located?** **A:** They are established in Medical Colleges, District Hospitals, Community Health Centres (CHCs), and some Primary Health Centres (PHCs) [Previous Turns].

**Q: How many tests are needed to confirm HIV in an adult?** **A:** Three different rapid tests must be reactive to declare a person HIV-positive.
