[[NUTRITION]]

The **Anemia Mukt Bharat (AMB)** strategy, originally launched in **2018** as part of the POSHAN Abhiyaan, has undergone a major transformation in **June 2026**. It is now formally renamed the **Anemia Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan**, shifting from a purely preventive supplementation model to a comprehensive, case-based management approach centred on **Testing, Treatment, and Digital Tracking**.

---

### **I. The Core Shift: From 6x6x6 to 7x7x7 Framework** ★ Important Exam Point

The most significant update is the expansion of the strategic matrix to include a seventh component in each category to address early-life vulnerabilities and improve accountability.

### **II. The T4 Strategy: A New Operational Paradigm** ★ Important Exam Point

The Abhiyaan has transitioned from the previous T3 approach (Test, Treat, Talk) to the **T4 Model**.

1. **Test:** Intensified screening using **Digital Invasive Haemoglobinometers** (point-of-care) and semi-auto analysers at health facilities.
2. **Treat:** Management of cases according to severity. A major update is the institutionalization of **Intravenous (IV) Iron Therapy**—specifically **Ferric Carboxymaltose (FCM)** and **Iron Sucrose**—for pregnant and lactating women who are severely anaemic or non-responsive to oral iron.
3. **Talk:** Targeted counselling on nutrition, improving compliance, and debunking myths (e.g., that IFA causes dark complexion in babies).
4. **Track (The New 4th 'T'):** Ensuring a strict continuum of care through digital monitoring of every identified case from screening to full recovery and follow-up.

---

### **III. Strategic Service Delivery Updates**

- **Delayed Cord Clamping:** Mandatory practice of delaying umbilical cord clamping for at least **3 minutes** (or until pulsations cease) at birth to improve infant iron reserves for up to 6 months.
- **Food Fortification:** Use of **fortified rice** (enriched with Iron, Folic Acid, and Vitamin B12) and **Double Fortified Salt** (Iron + Iodine) is now mandatory in public health programs like PM-POSHAN and ICDS.
- **Case-Based Management:** Shift from "distributing tablets" to "ensuring recovery," with systematic follow-up testing 4–6 weeks after initiating treatment.

---

### **IV. The Data Controversy: NFHS-6 vs. DABS-I**

A major recent update in public health policy is the **omission of anaemia prevalence data** from the **NFHS-6 (2023-24)**factsheet.

- **The Reason:** The Health Ministry disputed the previous NFHS-5 data (which showed rising anaemia), arguing that the **capillary blood method (finger-prick)** used in NFHS overestimates anaemia by diluting samples with interstitial fluid.
- **The New Standard:** Future anaemia estimations will be provided by the **Diet and Biomarkers Survey in India (DABS-I)** conducted by ICMR-NIN, which uses **venous blood sampling** (the global gold standard) for higher diagnostic accuracy.

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: What is the 7th intervention added in 2026?**
    - **A:** The "Eating Right" approach, focusing on dietary diversity and community mobilization (Jan Chetna).
- **Q: Why was anaemia data dropped from the NFHS-6 factsheet?**
    - **A:** Due to concerns over overestimation by the capillary (finger-prick) method; the government is shifting to venous blood sampling through DABS-I.
- **Q: What is the role of 'delayed cord clamping' in AMB?**
    - **A:** It is a low-cost intervention to provide an immediate iron boost to newborns, protecting them for the first 6 months of life.

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Using 6x6x6:** Many students still write the old "6x6x6" strategy. For full marks in 2026 exams, you must mention the **7x7x7 AMB Abhiyaan**.
- **Ignoring the 4th 'T':** Forgetting that "Track" has been added to the Test-Treat-Talk model.
- **Confusing Sampling Methods:** Not knowing the difference between capillary (NFHS) and venous (DABS-I) sampling and why the latter is more accurate.
