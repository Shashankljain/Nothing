[[NUTRITION]]
**Characteristics of the Reference Adult Indian Woman** ★ Important Exam Point

The 2024 updates from ICMR-NIN have revised these standards to reflect the current health profile of the nation.

|**Category**|**Characteristic / Value**|**Significance in Public Health**|
|---|---|---|
|**Age Group**|**19–39 years**|Represents the most productive and reproductive phase of the lifecycle.|
|**Body Weight**|**55 kg**|The standard weight used to calculate per-kg nutrient requirements (e.g., protein) [Turn 14, 1208].|
|**Height**|**1.61 metres**|Necessary for calculating the reference Body Mass Index (BMI).|
|**Activity Level**|**Sedentary**|The 2000 Kcal "My Plate" model is primarily designed for a sedentary woman.|
|**BMI**|**21.2** kg/m2|Falls within the "Normal Range" of 18.5 to 24.9 kg/m2.|
|**Health Status**|**Healthy / Normal**|Must be free from evident disease, showing normal physiological functioning (e.g., normal BP, pulse, and organ size).|

---

**III. Nutritional and Physiological Importance**

- **Calorie Reference:** The current nutritional guidelines for India focus on a **2000 Kcal/day** intake for the reference sedentary woman. This intake is designed to ensure the average value of all necessary macronutrients and micronutrients (minerals and vitamins) is met.
- **Macronutrient Distribution:** Her diet is planned to restrict total fat to **20–30%** and saturated fats to **<10%** of total energy to prevent Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) like obesity and CHD.
- **Assessment of "Normality":** The reference woman helps clinicians identify deviations from health. For example, if an adult woman’s weight remains more or less constant (within 2.3 kg) of her weight at age 25, it is considered a sign of physical health.
