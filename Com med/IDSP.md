[[Health programmes in India]]

The **Integrated Disease Surveillance Project (IDSP)** is a decentralized, state-based surveillance system in India intended to ==detect== and ==respond to disease outbreaks== early. It provides a common platform for the surveillance of various communicable and non-communicable diseases to ensure a timely and effective public health response.

---

### **I. Introduction**

- **Definition:** IDSP is a national health programme that aims to strengthen disease surveillance for both infectious and non-communicable diseases through a decentralized mechanism.
- **Launch Year:** It was officially launched in **2004**.
- **Public Health Significance:** By identifying early warning signals, the project helps prevent small clusters of cases from turning into large-scale epidemics.

### **II. Aim and Objectives**

The primary **aim** of IDSP is to strengthen the disease surveillance system for essential health data to be used in planning, implementing, and evaluating health services.

**Core Objectives:**

1. **Early Warning:** To provide early warning signals and initiate a rapid response to any health emergency.
2. **Decentralization:** To move surveillance from the national level to the state and district levels for faster action.
3. **Data Integrity:** To assess the quality and integrity of reported data and minimize percentage deviations from standard survey data.
4. **Capacity Building:** To train health workers in disease surveillance, control, and management of information systems.

---

### **III. Strategy and Reporting Mechanism** ★ Important Exam Point
## Surveillance methods
![[Pasted image 20260716093630.png]]
The IDSP relies on a systematic reporting structure involving different "forms" to capture data from various levels of the healthcare system.

- **P Form (Presumptive):** Used for reporting cases based on ==clinical suspicion== by *medical officers.*
- **L Form (Laboratory):** Used for reporting cases that have been confirmed by ==laboratory tests.== 
- **S Form (Syndromic):** _(Contextually implied in surveillance)_ Used by *paramedical staff/health workers* for reporting based on ==symptoms==.

**Weekly Fever Alerts:** The project is particularly vital for malaria control, where it provides weekly fever alerts that act as early warning signals for potential outbreaks.

---

### **IV. Organizational Structure**

The project operates across multiple tiers to ensure data flows from the periphery to the centre:

1. **National Level:** National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC), Delhi, which acts as the apex laboratory and nodal agency.
2. **State Level:** State Surveillance Units (SSU) responsible for state-specific roadmap development.
3. **District Level:** District Surveillance Units (DSU) which are the "==unit of planning==" for local interventions.
4. **Peripheral Level:** PHCs and Health and Wellness Centres (HWC) where initial case identification occurs.

---

### **V. Monitoring and Evaluation**

Monitoring under IDSP is a continuous process of observing, recording, and reporting activities to ensure they are on schedule.

**Key Indicators Used:** ★ Important Exam Point

- **Completeness of IDSP Reporting:** Measuring the proportion of P and L forms submitted on time.
- **Percentage Deviation:** Assessing data integrity by comparing reported data against standard survey results.
- **Case Notification Rate:** The number of new episodes notified per 100,000 population.

---

### **VI. Public Health Significance (Examples)**

- **Malaria:** IDSP's weekly alerts are a core supportive strategy for the **National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme (NVBDCP)**.
- **Cholera:** Surveillance is part of the integrated system to monitor acute watery diarrhoea and severe dehydration.
- **Zoonoses:** It supports the monitoring of zoonotic diseases like Kyasanur Forest Disease (KFD) and Rabies.

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: What is the main difference between the P form and L form?**
    - **A:** The P form is for presumptive cases (clinically diagnosed), while the L form is for laboratory-confirmed cases.
- **Q: How does IDSP help in malaria control?**
    - **A:** It provides weekly fever alerts that serve as early warning signals for potential outbreaks.
- **Q: What is the significance of the 2004 launch?**
    - **A:** It marked the shift toward an integrated, decentralized surveillance system in India.

---

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Confusing IDSP with NCDC:** IDSP is the _project/programme_, whereas NCDC is the _institution_ that manages it.
- **Forgetting the L Form:** Students often mention clinical diagnosis but forget that laboratory confirmation (L form) is a critical part of the data integrity check.
- **Ignoring Non-Communicable Diseases:** While famous for outbreaks, IDSP also encompasses the surveillance of NCD risk factors and conditions.
