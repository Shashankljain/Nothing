# National AIDS Control Programme (NACP)

### **1. Introduction and Definitions**

The **National AIDS Control Programme (NACP)** is a comprehensive national health programme launched by the Government of India to control the spread of **Acquired Immuno-Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS)**.

- **HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus):** A retrovirus that breaks down the body's immune system, specifically destroying **T4 helper cells (CD4+ cells)**, leaving the individual vulnerable to life-threatening opportunistic infections.
- **AIDS:** The end-stage of HIV infection, characterized by severe immune deficiency and the presence of "AIDS-defining" opportunistic infections or malignancies.
- **Epidemiological Status in India:** India's epidemic is marked by heterogeneity, meaning it is not a single epidemic but multiple distinct epidemics within states. It is primarily a **concentrated epidemic** driven by sexual transmission (86%), followed by parent-to-child and injecting drug use.

### **3. Aim and Goals**

- **Vision:** Vision Zero—**Zero new infections, Zero discrimination, and Zero AIDS-related deaths**.
- **Global Target:** To **end the AIDS epidemic as a public health threat by 2030** (SDG 3.3).
- **NACP Goal:** Reduce the annual number of new HIV infections and AIDS-related deaths by 80% compared to the 2010 baseline.

---

### **4. Key Targets: The 95-95-95 Strategy**

By 2030, the programme aims to achieve the following "Fast-Track" targets:

1. **95%** of all people living with HIV (PLHIV) should **know their HIV status**.
2. **95%** of all people diagnosed with HIV should **receive sustained antiretroviral therapy (ART)**.
3. **95%** of all people receiving ART should have **undetectable viral loads (viral suppression)**.

---

### **5. Programme Components and Strategies**

#### **A. Preventive Services (Primary Prevention)**

1. **Targeted Interventions (TI):** Focuses on "Key Populations" at high risk:
    - **Female Sex Workers (FSW)**.
    - **Men who have sex with men (MSM)**.
    - **Injecting Drug Users (IDU):** Includes needle-syringe exchange and **Opioid Substitution Therapy (OST)**.
    - **Transgender/Hijra persons**.
2. **Bridge Population Interventions:** Targeting groups that link high-risk groups to the general population, such as **Truckers** and **Migrants**.
3. **General Population Prevention:**
    - Information, Education, and Communication (IEC).
    - Condom promotion and provision.
    - **Blood Safety:** Mandatory screening of all donated blood for HIV 1 & 2.

#### **B. Diagnostic Services (The 5 Cs)**

Testing is provided through **Integrated Counselling and Testing Centres (ICTC)**. Testing must follow the **WHO "5Cs" principles**:

1. **Consent:** Voluntary and informed.
2. **Confidentiality**.
3. **Counselling:** Pre-test and post-test.
4. **Correct test results**.
5. **Connection:** Direct linkage to care and treatment.

#### **C. Care, Support, and Treatment (Secondary Prevention)**

1. **"Treat All" Policy:** India has adopted the policy to initiate ART for all PLHIV immediately upon diagnosis, regardless of their CD4 cell count.
2. **Antiretroviral Therapy (ART):**
    - **Regimen:** The preferred first-line regimen for adults and adolescents (>30kg) is **TLD** (Tenofovir 300mg + Lamivudine 300mg + Dolutegravir 50mg) as a single pill once a day.
    - **Goals of ART:** Clinical (survival), Virological (load suppression), and Immunological (CD4 recovery).
3. **PPTCT (Prevention of Parent-to-Child Transmission):** Now part of **EVTH** (Elimination of Vertical Transmission of HIV), ensuring all HIV-positive pregnant women receive lifelong ART.

#### **D. Management of Co-infections**

- **TB/HIV Co-infection:** TB is the leading cause of death among PLHIV. Strategies include bi-directional screening and **Isoniazid Preventive Therapy (IPT)**, which can reduce TB risk by up to 97% when given with ART.

---

### **6. Organizational Structure**

- **National Level:** **National AIDS Control Organization (NACO)** under the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare.
- **State Level:** **State AIDS Control Societies (SACS)**.
- **Facility Level:** ART Centres, ICTCs, and Link ART Centres.

---

### **★ High-Yield Exam Points**

- **Window Period:** The 2–12 week period after infection where a person is infectious but tests negative on standard antibody tests.
- **CD4 Threshold:** A CD4 count **< 200 cells/µL** is a critical predictor for progression to AIDS-defining illnesses.
- **Confirmatory Test:** **Western Blot** is the gold standard used to confirm a repeatedly reactive ELISA.
- **Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP):** Must be started within **72 hours** of exposure (preferably within hours) and continued for **4 weeks**.

---

### **Flowchart: Management of a Newly Diagnosed PLHIV**

1. **Confirmation of HIV status** (via ICTC using 3 rapid tests).
2. **Clinical Staging** (WHO Stages 1–4).
3. **Readiness Assessment** and Counselling.
4. **Baseline Investigations** (CD4 count, Viral load, screening for TB/Diabetes).
5. **Immediate ART Initiation** (TLD regimen).
6. **Monitoring** (CD4 count every 6 months, monthly clinical follow-up).

---

### **Common Student Mistakes**

- **Denominator Confusion:** Confusing the targets for 95-95-95. Remember: 95% of _total_ PLHIV $\rightarrow$ 95% of _diagnosed_ on treatment $\rightarrow$ 95% of _treated_ virally suppressed.
- **Confusing Screening vs. Diagnostic:** Thinking ELISA is confirmatory. **ELISA is for screening**; Western Blot or molecular tests are for confirmation.
- **Overlooking the Window Period:** Forgetting that a negative test in a high-risk individual might simply mean they are in the window period.

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

**Q: What is the "Treat All" recommendation?** **A:** It means starting ART in all HIV-positive individuals regardless of their CD4 count or clinical stage to reduce morbidity and transmission.

**Q: Name the preferred first-line ART regimen in India.** **A:** TLD (Tenofovir + Lamivudine + Dolutegravir).

**Q: What is the significance of the 5 Cs?** **A:** They are the ethical and clinical pillars of HIV testing: Consent, Confidentiality, Counselling, Correct results, and Connection to care.

**Q: What is the "Window Period"?** **A:** It is the time (2-12 weeks) between HIV infection and the point when antibodies become detectable by standard tests. The person is highly infectious during this time.
