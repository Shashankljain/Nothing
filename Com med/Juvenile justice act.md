The **Juvenile Justice Act (JJ Act)** is the primary legal framework in India for the care, protection, and rehabilitation of children who are either in conflict with the law or in need of care and protection. It represents a shift from a punitive approach to a "Social Defence" and rehabilitative model.

---

### **I. Introduction and Definitions**

- **Definition of a Juvenile Delinquent:** A minor who shows a deviation from normal behavior and has committed an offense punishable by law, such as theft, sexual offenses, murder, or burglary.
- **Definition of the Act:** The Juvenile Justice Act is a specialized legislative measure designed to provide for the care, protection, maintenance, training, and rehabilitation of delinquent children.
- **Social Context:** Delinquency is viewed as a **social problem** or **social pathology** resulting from social maladjustment, poverty, broken homes, and modern ways of living.

---

### **II. History and Milestones** ★ Important Exam Point

The legislative journey of juvenile justice in India has evolved to meet international standards.

- **The Children Act, 1960:** The precursor to the JJ Act, providing the first specialized approach for children in India.
- **Juvenile Justice Act, 1986:** Enacted to create a uniform legal framework across all states.
- **Juvenile Justice Act, 2015:** The most recent major revision, enacted to address emerging challenges in juvenile crime and care.

---

### **III. Why is the Act Necessary? (Objectives)**

1. **Rehabilitation over Punishment:** To treat the "offended" child rather than punish them like an adult criminal.
2. **Social Defence:** To defend society against criminality by creating conditions conducive to a healthy and wholesome growth of human life.
3. **Holistic Care:** To address the "mental deprivation" caused by broken homes or "problem families".
4. **Specialization:** To provide a separate justice system that recognizes the "stormy" psychological transitions of adolescence.

---

### **IV. Institutional Infrastructure under the Act** ★ Important Exam Point

The Act mandates specific institutions to ensure children are kept away from the adult criminal justice system.

|Institution|Function|
|:--|:--|
|**Juvenile/Children’s Courts**|Specialized courts to hear cases involving children in a non-threatening environment.|
|**Child Welfare Boards**|Bodies responsible for children "in need of care and protection" (e.g., street children, orphans).|
|**Remand Homes**|Protective facilities for the temporary stay of a child while their case is being investigated.|
|**Certified Schools**|Institutions for the long-term education and vocational training of delinquent children.|
|**After-care Facilities**|Support systems to help the child reintegrate into society and prevent a relapse into crime after release.|

---

### **V. Strategy and Implementation**

- **Nodal Agency:** The **National Institute of Social Defence (1975)** coordinates these services under the Department of Social Welfare.
- **Personnel:** Health visitors, midwives, social workers, and medical officers of health play a role in rehabilitating "problem families" to prevent delinquency.
- **Integration:** The Act is supported by other social schemes like **RBSK** (Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram) which screens children for developmental delays and diseases that may contribute to behavioral issues.

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points** ★

- **Core Legislation:** Mention the **JJ Act 1986** and its latest version, **JJ Act 2015**.
- **Social Pathology:** Delinquency is often linked to **broken homes** (separation/death of parents) and **mental deprivation**.
- **Infrastructure:** Remember the triad of **Juvenile Courts**, **Remand Homes**, and **After-care facilities**.
- **Rehabilitation:** The goal is the physical and mental restoration of the child so they can resume their place in society.

---

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

- **Q: How does the JJ Act differ from the IPC (Indian Penal Code)?**
    - **A:** The IPC focuses on punishment and deterrence for adults, while the JJ Act focuses on **rehabilitation**, education, and the "best interest of the child".
- **Q: What is a "Remand Home"?**
    - **A:** It is a temporary stay facility where a child is kept safely during the inquiry process to avoid contact with hardened criminals in jails.
- **Q: Who is a "child" under the current JJ Act?**
    - **A:** A person who has not completed **18 years** of age.

---

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**

- **Using the word "Jail":** Never use the word "jail" for juveniles; the correct terms are **Remand Homes** or **Certified Schools**.
- **Forgetting the Precursor:** Students often forget to mention **The Children Act, 1960**, which laid the foundation for juvenile justice in India.
- **Ignoring the Social Aspect:** Do not just focus on the law; explain the **social causes** (poverty, urbanization) that make the Act necessary.
