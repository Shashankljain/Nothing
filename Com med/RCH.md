[[Health programmes in India]]
The concepts of family planning and family welfare represent a significant shift in India’s public health approach, moving from a narrow focus on "population control" to a holistic view of the family's health.

---

### **1. Difference Between Family Planning and Family Welfare**

While the terms are often used interchangeably, they differ significantly in scope and philosophy.

|**Feature**|**Family Planning**|**Family Welfare**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Definition**|The ability of individuals/couples to anticipate and attain their desired number of children and plan the spacing/timing of births.|A comprehensive concept that includes family planning as one component but extends to the overall well-being and health of the family.|
|**Focus**|Primarily on **contraception**, spacing, and limiting family size.|Focuses on maternal health, child survival, nutrition, and environmental sanitation.|
|**Approach**|Clinical and technical; focused on "target-oriented" goals in the early years.|Social and community-oriented; "target-free" and need-based.|
|**Historical Context**|India was the first to launch the "National Family Planning Control Programme" in 1952.|The program was renamed "National Family Welfare Programme" in **1997** to reflect this broader focus.|
|**Goal**|To achieve population stabilization by reducing birth rates.|To improve the quality of life of the family as a unit.|

### **2. Reproductive and Child Health (RCH) Program**

The RCH program was launched in **1997** (Phase I) and **2005** (Phase II). It integrated earlier programs like the Child Survival and Safe Motherhood (CSSM) program into a unified framework.

#### **A. Components of RCH**

The program is built on four primary pillars:

1. **Maternal Health:** Ensuring safe pregnancy and delivery.
2. **Child Health:** Focus on child survival, immunization, and nutrition.
3. **Family Planning:** Providing a "cafeteria choice" of contraceptive methods.
4. **RTI/STI Management:** Prevention and treatment of Reproductive Tract and Sexually Transmitted Infections.
5. **Adolescent Health (RMNCH+A):** Added later to address the unique needs of the 10–19 age group.

#### **B. Key Strategies**

- **Decentralised Planning:** Moving from top-down targets to a **"Bottom-Up Approach"** where targets are based on community needs.
- **Institutional Strengthening:** Upgrading First Referral Units (FRUs) and PHCs to provide 24/7 obstetric and neonatal care.
- **Skilled Birth Attendance (SBA):** Ensuring every delivery is attended by a trained professional (ANM/SN/Doctor).
- **Public-Private Mix:** Involving NGOs and private practitioners to increase service reach.
- **Incentivization:** Using schemes like **Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)** to encourage institutional deliveries.

### **3. Maternal Health Services Under RCH**

The services are designed to follow the **"Maternity Cycle"** from registration to postnatal care.

#### **A. Antenatal Care (ANC) Services**

- **Early Registration:** Pregnant women should be registered within the **first 12 weeks** of pregnancy by the ANM.
- **Antenatal Check-ups:** A minimum of **4 visits** are mandatory (ideally 13–14 visits).
- **Investigations:** Monitoring weight gain (target: 10–12 kg), blood pressure (to detect eclampsia), and hemoglobin (to detect anemia).
- **Immunization:** Two doses of Tetanus Toxoid (TT) or Tetanus-diphtheria (Td) vaccine.
- **Supplementation:** Provision of Iron & Folic Acid (IFA) tablets for 180 days to prevent maternal anemia.

#### **B. Intranatal (Delivery) Services**

- **Institutional Delivery:** Promoting birth in facilities equipped for emergencies.
- **Safe Home Delivery:** If institutional delivery is not possible, providing **Disposable Delivery Kits** to trained birth attendants.
- **Emergency Obstetric Care (EmOC):** FRUs are strengthened with blood storage facilities and surgical capacity (C-sections).

#### **C. Postnatal Care (PNC) Services**

- **Follow-up Visits:** A minimum of **3 postnatal checkups** are recommended.
- **Post-delivery Stay:** Encouraging a mandatory **48-hour stay** in the facility to monitor for complications like PPH (Postpartum Hemorrhage).
- **Lactation Support:** Counseling on early initiation of breastfeeding (within 1 hour) and exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months.
- **Postpartum Contraception:** Providing options like Postpartum IUCD (PPIUCD) or sterilization.

### **★ High-Yield Exam Points**

- **The "Switch":** Remember the 1997 name change from Family Planning to Family Welfare.
- **JSY vs. JSSK:** JSY is for **cash assistance**; JSSK (2011) makes the delivery and treatment **absolutely free** for the mother and sick newborn.
- **RCH Phase II Goal:** To achieve a **Total Fertility Rate (TFR) of 2.1**.

### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **RCH Definition:** Integrated approach for maternal/child health and family welfare.
- **Maternal Services:** ANC (Register/Supplement), Intranatal (SBA/Institutional), PNC (Checkups/Breastfeeding).
- **Strategy:** Shift from "Targets" to "Needs" and from "Control" to "Welfare."
- **Mnemonic for ANC Services:** **W**eight, **B**P, **H**emoglobin, **I**FA, **T**T (**W-B-H-I-T**).

### **Frequently Asked Viva Questions**

**Q: What is a "First Referral Unit" (FRU)?** **A:** A facility (usually a CHC) equipped to handle obstetric emergencies, including C-sections and blood transfusions.

**Q: What is the significance of the 48-hour stay?** **A:** Most maternal deaths due to hemorrhage or sepsis occur within the first 48 hours; monitoring during this period is life-saving.

**Q: Who is responsible for registering ANC in a village?** **A:** The Auxiliary Nurse Midwife (ANM) with the help of the ASHA.
