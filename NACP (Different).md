# National AIDS Control Programme (NACP)

### **Introduction**
The National AIDS Control Programme (NACP) is a comprehensive centrally sponsored scheme launched by the Government of India to control the spread of HIV and manage AIDS in the country. 

*   **HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus):** A lentivirus that breaks down the body’s immune system, leaving the individual vulnerable to life-threatening opportunistic infections and malignancies.
*   **AIDS (Acquired Immuno-Deficiency Syndrome):** The end-stage of HIV infection, characterized by a low CD4 count (usually <200 cells/µL) and the presence of opportunistic infections. ★ **Important Exam Point: AIDS is a clinical stage, not just the presence of the virus.**

---

### **Aim and Objectives**
**Aim:** To pave the way for an **"AIDS-free India"**.

**Core Objectives:**
1.  **Reduce Spread:** To halt and reverse the spread of HIV infection in India.
2.  **Reduce Morbidity & Mortality:** To reduce the sickness and deaths associated with HIV/AIDS.
3.  **Long-term Response:** To strengthen India’s capacity to respond to the epidemic on a long-term basis.

---

### **Phases of NACP**
The programme has evolved through several phases:

| Phase | Period | Salient Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase I** | 1987–1999 | Focused on awareness, establishing structures like NACO, and ensuring blood safety. |
| **Phase II** | 1999–2006 | Became 100% centrally sponsored. Shifted focus from awareness to **behaviour change**. Decentralized services to states. |
| **Phase III** | 2007–2012 | Aimed at **halting and reversing the epidemic**. Saturation of high-risk group coverage through Targeted Interventions (TI). |
| **NSP (Recent)**| 2017–2024 | National Strategic Plan focusing on the "Three Zeros" and 95-95-95 targets. |

---

### **Strategy: Objectives vs Strategies**
The NACP uses a multi-pronged strategy divided into Prevention, Care, and Surveillance.

| Objective Area | Strategies |
| :--- | :--- |
| **High-Risk Groups** | **Targeted Interventions (TI):** Peer counselling, condom promotion, and STI treatment for FSWs, IDUs, and MSMs. |
| **Low-Risk Population**| Holistic IEC (Information, Education, Communication), safe blood, and voluntary counselling/testing. |
| **Care and Support** | Providing free ART (Antiretroviral Therapy), managing HIV-TB co-infection, and Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP). |
| **Surveillance** | Annual sentinel surveillance and mapping of high-risk populations. |

---

### **Beneficiaries**
The programme prioritises populations based on their risk level:
1.  **Highest Risk Groups (HRG):** Commercial Sex Workers (FSW), Homosexuals (MSM), and Injecting Drug Users (IDU).
2.  **Bridge Populations:** People who link HRGs to the general population, such as truckers, migrant labourers, and street children.
3.  **General Population:** Including pregnant women (for PPTCT) and youth.

---

### **Components of the Programme**

#### **1. Targeted Interventions (TI)**
Identification of high-risk groups to provide peer counselling, condom promotion, and treatment for Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs).

#### **2. STI/RTI Management**
STIs are "co-factors" for HIV transmission. The programme uses the **Syndromic Approach** for management, which is simple, feasible, and effective at the primary health care level.

#### **3. Condom Promotion**
Nearly 85% of HIV infections in India occur through unprotected multi-partner sexual contact.
*   **Strategy:** Social marketing of condoms and ensuring availability of high-quality (lubricated) condoms.

#### **4. Blood Safety**
*   **Policy:** Professional blood donation has been prohibited since 1998; only voluntary donation is encouraged.
*   **Mandatory Testing:** All units must be tested for HIV, Hepatitis B & C, Syphilis, and Malaria.

#### **5. Voluntary Counselling and Testing (VCT/ICTC)**
Integrated Counselling and Testing Centres (ICTCs) are the "gateway" to HIV services.
*   **Activities:** Pre-test counselling, HIV testing (using at least 3 different antigens/tests), and Post-test counselling (breaking the news gently if positive).

#### **6. Prevention of Parent to Child Transmission (PPTCT)**
*   **Primary Strategy:** Identifying HIV-positive mothers in the antenatal period and referring them to VCTC.
*   **Secondary Strategy:** 
    *   **Antiretroviral Therapy:** Starting ART (usually Zidovudine) from the 2nd trimester.
    *   **Safe Delivery:** Elective C-section (where safe) and avoiding invasive procedures.
    *   **Infant Care:** Administering Nevirapine syrup to the newborn within 72 hours and follow-up until 18 months.

7) Diagnostic Services (The 5 Cs)**

Testing is provided through **Integrated Counselling and Testing Centres (ICTC)**. Testing must follow the **WHO "5Cs" principles**:

1. **Consent:** Voluntary and informed.
2. **Confidentiality**.
3. **Counselling:** Pre-test and post-test.
4. **Correct test results**.
5. **Connection:** Direct linkage to care and treatment.

### **Organizational Structure**
*   **Central:** National AIDS Control Organisation (**NACO**), established in 1992 under the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare.
*   **State:** State AIDS Control Societies (**SACS**).
*   **Peripheral:** ICTCs, ART centres, and STD clinics.

---

### **Monitoring and Evaluation: HIV Sentinel Surveillance**
Sentinel surveillance identifies missing cases to estimate prevalence in the total population.
*   **Strategy:** Annual surveys at the same "sentinel sites" (e.g., STD clinics, ANC clinics) using **Unlinked Anonymous Testing**.
*   **Classification of States:**
    *   **Group I (High Prevalence):** >5% prevalence in HRGs and ≥1% in ANC women.
    *   **Group II (Moderate):** >5% in HRGs but <1% in ANC women.
    *   **Group III (Low):** <5% in HRGs and <1% in ANC women.

---

### **Recent Updates: National Strategic Plan (2017–2024)**
The vision is to achieve **"Three Zeros"**:
1.  Zero new infections.
2.  Zero AIDS-related deaths.
3.  Zero discrimination.

**Targets by 2024 (95-95-95):**
*   **95%** of people living with HIV know their status.
*   **95%** of those who know their status are on ART.
*   **95%** of those on ART achieve effective viral load suppression.

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points**
*   ★ **Window Period:** The 2–12 week interval after infection where antibodies are not yet detectable, but the person is highly infectious.
*   ★ **3 by 5 Initiative:** A global goal (WHO/UNAIDS) to provide ART to 3 million people in developing countries by the end of 2005.
*   ★ **ICTC vs VCTC:** ICTC is the modern term for centres providing integrated counselling and testing for both HIV and other conditions like TB.

---

### **Common Mistakes Students Make**
*   **Confusing HIV and AIDS:** Remember, HIV is the virus; AIDS is the final clinical stage.
*   **Mandatory Testing:** Students often think testing is mandatory. Under NACP, testing **must be voluntary** with informed consent to protect human rights.
*   **Cure Claims:** Never write that ART cures HIV. It only **controls** the virus and prolongs life.
