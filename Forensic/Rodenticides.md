### **Rodenticides (Rat Poisons)**
[[Agricultural poison]]
---

#### **1. Basic Concepts**

**Definition**  
**Rodenticides** are a group of diverse chemical agents specifically designed and used to control or kill rodent populations (rats, mice, squirrels). In forensic toxicology, they are classified under **Miscellaneous Agrochemicals**.

**Introduction**  
In India, rodenticide poisoning is a major medical and forensic concern due to its easy availability in households and agricultural settings . They range from metallic salts to highly toxic phosphide-releasing compounds.

**Classification of Common Rodenticides**

|**Type**|**Common Agents**|**Key Characteristic**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Phosphides**|Aluminum Phosphide (AlPhos), Zinc Phosphide|Releases **Phosphine gas**; Garlic odor .|
|**Non-metallic Irritants**|Yellow Phosphorus|Luminescent 'smoking' vomit; Jaundice.|
|**Spinal Neurotics**|Strychnine (_Strychnos nux-vomica_)|Risus sardonicus; Conscious seizures.|
|**Heavy Metals**|Arsenic Trioxide, Thallium|Odorless/Tasteless; Cumulative toxicity.|
|**Inorganic Salts**|Barium Carbonate|Muscle poison; Causes hypokalemia.|

#### **2. Specific Rodenticides of Medico-Legal Importance**

#### **A. Aluminum Phosphide (AlPhos / QuickPhos)**

★ **Important Exam Point: The commonest rodenticide poisoning in Northern India .**

- **Mechanism:** Liberates **Phosphine gas ($PH_3$)** upon contact with moisture or stomach HCl . It is a protoplasmic poison that inhibits mitochondrial cytochrome C oxidase, leading to ATP depletion and cell death .
- **Signs:** Severe metabolic acidosis, refractory shock, and a characteristic **garlic or decaying fish-like odor** .
- **Diagnosis:** **Silver Nitrate Test** (Filter paper turns black when exposed to gastric aspirate/breath) .
- **Management:** No specific antidote; intensive supportive care including gastric lavage with $KMnO_4$ (1:10,000) and administration of coconut oil to inhibit gas release .

#### **B. Zinc Phosphide**

- **Properties:** A dark gray powder used as a common household rodenticide.
- **Action:** Similar to AlPhos, it releases phosphine gas in acidic conditions.
- **Features:** Metallic taste, vomiting, and garlicky smell.

#### **C. Yellow Phosphorus (Ratol Paste)**

- **Clinical Stages:**
    1. **First:** GI irritation, garlic odor, **luminescent vomit**.
    2. **Second:** "Symptom-free" period (1–4 days).
    3. **Third:** Liver failure (jaundice) and multi-organ dysfunction.
- **Management:** **NO oils/fats (demulcents)** as phosphorus is lipid-soluble and absorption increases.

#### **D. Strychnine (Nux-vomica)**

- **Symptoms:** Choking sensation, **Risus sardonicus** (evil grin), and **Opisthotonus** (arch-like hyperextension of the body).
- **Key Feature:** The patient remains **fully conscious** during convulsions.

---

#### **3. Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Suicide:** Most common manner in India due to cheap agricultural availability .
- **Accidental:** Often occurs in children who mistake rat paste for toothpaste or chew on contaminated items.
- **Homicide:** Rare for phosphides due to strong odors, but **Arsenic** and **Thallium** are ideal homicidal agents because they are tasteless and odorless.
- **Stupefying Agent:** Some rodenticides like AlPhos have been used in chemical terrorism .

#### **5. Specimen Collection & Preservation**

★ **Important Exam Point: Preservation of Viscera**

- **Routine:** Stomach (entire), small intestine (30 cm), liver (500g), and kidney (half).
- **Preservative:** **Saturated solution of common salt** is most common.
- **Note:** **Rectified spirit is contraindicated** for phosphorus (Mnemonic: **PAPPA** - **P**henol, **A**lcohol, **P**hosphorus, **P**araldehyde, **A**cetic acid).

---

#### **6. High-Yield Exam Points**

- **Garlic Odor:** Phosphorus, Arsenic, and Phosphides.
- **Phossy Jaw:** Chronic phosphorus poisoning causing necrosis of the lower jaw.
- **Silver Nitrate Test:** Diagnostic bedside test for phosphine gas .
- **Luminescent Vomit:** Diagnostic of Yellow Phosphorus ingestion.
- **Antidote for Strychnine:** Barbiturates (Sodium Amytal/Thiopental).

#### **7. Viva Questions**

1. **Why is AlPhos called "QuickPhos"?**
    - _Ans:_ Because it releases phosphine gas very rapidly once it hits the stomach acid .
2. **Why are oily demulcents contraindicated in phosphorus poisoning?**
    - _Ans:_ Phosphorus is lipid-soluble; oils facilitate its absorption into the system.
3. **What is the 'Broken Neck Sign' equivalent in Strychnine?**
    - _Ans:_ Opisthotonus (arch-like bending). (Note: Broken neck sign is specific to neurotoxic snakebite).
4. **Which rodenticide can be detected in a decomposed body?**
    - _Ans:_ Arsenic, Thallium, and Strychnine.

#### **8. Quick Revision Summary**

1. **Commonest:** AlPhos (Celphos) - releases Phosphine gas .
2. **Identify by Smell:** Garlic (Phosphorus, AlPhos, Zinc Phosphide).
3. **Test:** Silver Nitrate paper (turns black) .
4. **Autopsy:** Garlic odor and red velvety stomach mucosa (if Arsenic is the base).
5. **Duty:** Notify police under **Section 33 BNSS**.
