[[Laws]]
### **Human Organ Transplantation Act (THOTA), 1994**

---

#### **Basic Concepts**

**Introduction** The **Transplantation of Human Organs and Tissues Act (THOTA)** was enacted in **1994** to provide a legal framework for the removal, storage, and transplantation of human organs and tissues for therapeutic purposes. It aims to prevent commercial dealings (organ trafficking) which were rampant before its enactment.

**Historical Background**

- **1994:** Original Act passed.
- **2011:** Significant **Amendment Act** passed.
- **2014:** New rules (**THOT Rules**) came into force to implement the amendments.

**Objectives**

1. To regulate the removal and storage of human organs and tissues.
2. To prevent illegal commercial dealings in human organs.
3. To provide legal recognition for **Brainstem Death**.

**Key Definitions (★ Important Exam Point)**

- **Donor:** Any person at least **18 years of age** who voluntarily authorises the removal of their organs/tissues for therapeutic purposes.
- **Recipient:** A person into whom any human organ or tissue is proposed to be transplanted.
- **Near Relative:** Expanded in 2011 to include spouse, son, daughter, father, mother, brother, sister, grandparents, and grandchildren.
- **Brainstem Death:** The stage at which functions of the brainstem have permanently and irreversibly ceased.

---

#### **Types of Organ Donation**

|**Type**|**Details**|
|:--|:--|
|**Living Donation**|From "Near Relatives" or others with approval from the Authorisation Committee.|
|**Cadaveric Donation**|Organs harvested during the **Supravital period** after death.|
|**Swap Donation**|When a donor and recipient are near relatives but do not medically match, they can "swap" with another similar pair.|

---

#### **Brain Death Certification**

★ **Important Exam Point: Legal Death** In India, "death" is now legally considered **brainstem death**, allowing organs to be harvested while the heart is still beating via life support (beating-heart cadaver).

**Certification Board:** Must consist of four medical experts:

1. The medical practitioner in charge of the hospital.
2. An independent specialist (Neurologist/Neurosurgeon) nominated from a government-approved panel.
3. The doctor treating the patient.
4. Another specialist (Intensivist/Anesthetist).

**Tests Required:**

- Absence of brainstem reflexes (Pupillary, Corneal, Gag, and Vestibulo-ocular reflexes).
- **Apnoea test:** Patient cannot breathe spontaneously when disconnected from the ventilator.
- Tests must be repeated after **6 to 24 hours** to ensure irreversibility.

---

#### **Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Authorization:** Removal of organs is legal only when authorized by the donor (before death) or by the person in legal possession of the body (if no objection was raised by the deceased).
- **The Authorisation Committee:** A statutory body that reviews cases where the donor is NOT a "near relative" to ensure no money has changed hands.
- **Unclaimed Bodies:** Organs can be removed from unclaimed bodies in hospitals if not claimed within **48 hours**.

---

#### **Offences and Punishments (★ Important Exam Point)**

THOTA carries heavy penalties to deter organ commerce:

|**Offence**|**Punishment**|
|:--|:--|
|**Unauthorised removal of organs**|Imprisonment up to **10 years** and fine up to **₹20 Lakhs**.|
|**Unauthorised removal of tissues**|Imprisonment up to **3 years** and fine up to **₹5 Lakhs**.|
|**Commercial dealings in organs**|Imprisonment **5–10 years** and fine **₹20 Lakhs to ₹1 Crore**.|
|**Doctor's Professional Misconduct**|Name erased from medical register for **3 years** (1st offence) and **permanently**(subsequent offence).|

---

#### **Ethical Issues**

- **First Rule of Medicine:** "Do no harm"—donor surgery carries risks for a healthy person.
- **Informed Consent:** Must be voluntary and based on full disclosure of risks.
- **Commercialisation:** Concern that the poor may be exploited for their organs.

---

#### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **THOTA 1994:** Regulates removal, storage, and transplant.
- ★ **Near Relatives:** Includes grandparents and grandchildren (2011 amendment).
- ★ **Brain Death Board:** Consists of **4 members**.
- ★ **Beating-heart donor:** Donor whose brain is dead but circulation is maintained by machines.
- ★ **Swap Donation:** Allowed for unmatched pairs of near relatives.

#### **Viva Questions**

1. **What is the minimum age for a living organ donor?**
    - _Ans:_ 18 years.
2. **Can a doctor in the transplant team certify brain death?**
    - _Ans:_ No. Such tests should not be performed by members of the transplant team to avoid conflict of interest.
3. **What is a "Tissue Bank"?**
    - _Ans:_ A facility registered for the recovery, screening, testing, processing, storage, and distribution of tissues.
4. **What is the "Migenstrasse"?** (Note: Unrelated to THOTA, but found in sources).
    - _Ans:_ The path along the lesser curvature of the stomach followed by liquids/acids.

---

#### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **Act:** THOTA 1994 (amended 2011).
- **Legal Focus:** Regulates transplants and bans commercial organ sales.
- **Brain Death:** Legally "Death" in India; certified by a 4-member board twice.
- **Consent:** Donor (>18y) or legal heir.
- **Penalties:** Up to 10 years prison and ₹1 Crore fine for commercial trafficking.
