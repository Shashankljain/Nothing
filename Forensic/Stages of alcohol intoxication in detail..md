### **Alcohol Intoxication: Stages and Clinical Features**

---

#### **1. Basic Concepts**

**Definition**  
Alcohol intoxication is a clinical state resulting from the consumption of ethanol (ethyl alcohol), which acts as a progressive **Central Nervous System (CNS) depressant**.

**Introduction**  
Ethanol is a volatile, colorless liquid. Although many perceive it as a stimulant because it reduces social inhibitions, it actually depresses the specialized cells of the cerebral cortex that regulate judgment and self-criticism. As the dose increases, it depresses vital centers in the medulla, leading to coma and death.

**Classification of Stages**  
The effects of alcohol are conventionally divided into three distinct clinical stages based on the Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC).

|**Stage**|**BAC (mg/dL or mg%)**|**Key Characteristics**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**I. Stage of Excitement**|50 – 150 mg%|Euphoria, loss of inhibition, increased confidence.|
|**II. Stage of Incoordination**|150 – 250 mg%|Slurred speech, staggering gait, vomiting.|
|**III. Stage of Narcosis / Coma**|> 250 mg%|Deep sleep/coma, respiratory depression, potential death.|

---

#### **2. Detailed Stages of Intoxication**

##### **I. Stage of Excitement (BAC: 50–150 mg%)**

- **Behavioral Changes:** The person becomes euphoric with an exaggerated sense of well-being. They may become talkative, disclose secrets (known as *==**In vino veritas**==*—in wine there is truth), and behave recklessly.
- **Cognitive Effects:** Judgment is impaired, and the faculty of attention deteriorates.
- **Physical Signs:** Lowering of visual acuity and the presence of **nystagmus** (involuntary rhythmic eye movement).
- **Sexual Effect:** It increases sexual desire but markedly impairs physical performance.
- Pupils dilated

##### **II. Stage of Incoordination (BAC: 150–250 mg%)**

- **Mental State:** The person may become morose, irritable, or ill-tempered, depending on their underlying dominant impulses.
- **Motor Incoordination:** There is marked clumsiness and alterations in speech (slurring). The person is unable to perform skilled movements.
- **Biological Signs:**
    - **Nausea and vomiting** occur once alcohol reachers ~20% concentration in the intestines.
    - The face appears **flushed** due to vasodilation.
    - Pupils are dilated and react sluggishly to light.
- **Sensory Loss:** Sense of touch, taste, smell, and hearing are diminished.

##### **III. Stage of Narcosis / Coma (BAC: >250 mg%)**

- **Clinical Presentation:** The person becomes profoundly giddy, staggers, and eventually falls into a deep sleep from which they cannot be easily roused.
- ***==McEwan's Sign==* (★ Important Exam Point):** The pupils are characteristically ==constricted==, but if the patient is stimulated (e.g., by pinching or slapping), the ***pupils dilate with slow return***. This helps differentiate alcoholic coma from other types of coma.
- **Vital Signs:** Breathing depression -Breathing becomes stertorous (heavy snoring sound),
(circulatory depression )the pulse is rapid, and the body temperature falls (hypothermia).
- **Lethal Level:** Death typically occurs if the BAC is between **400–500 mg/dL**.

---

#### **3. Relevant Legal Concepts**

- **Mellanby Effect:** The clinical effects of alcohol are more pronounced when the blood level is **rising** than when it is falling at the same concentration.
- **Alcohol Blackout:** An episode of temporary anterograde amnesia where the person forgets events that occurred during the drinking session.
- **Pathological Intoxication:** A condition where even a small dose of alcohol produces an immediate and violent psychotic reaction in certain individuals.

---

#### **4. Relevant Law (BNS & BNSS, 2023)**

University examinations now require the application of the new 2023 criminal laws.

|**Provision**|**2023 Section**|**Medico-Legal Significance**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Involuntary Drunkenness**|**Section 22 BNS**|A person is not criminally responsible if the alcohol was administered without their knowledge or against their will.|
|**Voluntary Drunkenness**|**Section 22 BNS**|Voluntary intoxication is **NOT** an excuse for committing a crime.|
|**Public Misconduct**|**Section 355 BNS**|Punishment for a drunken person causing a disturbance in public.|
|**Medical Examination**|**Section 51/52 BNSS**|An accused can be examined by a doctor at the request of the police, using force if necessary, including taking blood/urine samples.|

---

#### **5. Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Criminal Responsibility:** Under Section 22 BNS, the key is whether the intoxication was self-induced or not.
- **Drunken Driving:** In India, the legal limit for driving is **30 mg/dL**.
- **Allegations of Negligence:** Doctors must diligently rule out head injuries or hypoglycemia before certifying someone as "just drunk," as these can mimic intoxication.

---

#### **6. Medico-Legal Examination (Drunkenness)**

★ **Important Exam Point: *==Field Impairment Tests (FITs)==***:

1. **Romberg Test:** Standing still with eyes closed and head tilted back; positive if the patient sways or falls.
2. **Walk and Turn Test:** Walking nine heel-to-toe steps along a straight line.
3. **One Leg Stand:** Raising one foot 6 inches off the ground while counting.
4. **Finger-Nose Test:** Touching the tip of the nose with the index finger with eyes closed.

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Earliest Sign:** Judgment impairment and loss of self-criticism.
- ★ **McEwan's Sign:** Pupils dilate on painful stimuli in alcoholic coma.
- ★ **Widmark’s Formula:** Used to calculate the total amount of alcohol absorbed in the body ($a = cpr$).
- ★ **Legal Limit:** 30 mg/100 mL of blood for drivers in India.

### **Viva Questions**

1. **What is the "Stage of 11 Ds"?**
    - _Ans:_ This refers to **Dhatura poisoning**, not alcohol. Students often confuse them because both cause a "drunken gait".
2. **Why is the brain opened first in an alcohol autopsy?**
    - _Ans:_ To detect the odor of alcohol before it is masked by other odors from the thorax or abdomen.
3. **What is the "Double-condom" sign?**
    - _Ans:_ A radiological rim of air seen in "Body Packers" who swallow drug packets (often cocaine or heroin) for smuggling.
