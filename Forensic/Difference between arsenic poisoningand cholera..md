### **Difference Between Arsenic Poisoning and Cholera**
[[Metallic poisoning]]

#### **1. Basic Concepts**

**Introduction**  
Acute arsenic poisoning is often referred to as the "great mimic" because its clinical presentation, particularly the gastrointestinal (GIT) symptoms, closely resembles **Cholera**, a natural bacterial infection caused by _Vibrio cholerae_.

**Medico-Legal Significance**  
★ **Important Exam Point: Ideal Homicidal Poison**  
Arsenic was historically known as the **"Inheritance Powder"** because it was used to kill family members for property. Its ability to simulate a natural disease like cholera allowed many homicides to go undetected by physicians, especially during cholera epidemics.

---

### **Differential Diagnosis: Comparison Table**

University examinations frequently ask for this specific differentiation in a 5-mark or 10-mark format.

|**Feature**|**Arsenic Poisoning (Acute)**|**Cholera (Infection)**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**GIT Symptoms (Order)**|**Pain in throat** $\rightarrow$ Vomiting $\rightarrow$ Purging.|**Purging** $\rightarrow$ Vomiting $\rightarrow$ Pain in throat.|
|**Vomitus**|Contains mucus, bile, and often **blood**.|Watery; usually lacks mucus, bile, or blood.|
|**Stools (Purging)**|Rice-watery; may contain **blood**.|Rice-watery; **no blood**; passed in a continuous involuntary jet.|
|**Tenesmus**|**Present** (Persistent, painful desire to empty bowels).|**Absent**.|
|**Throat Pain**|Occurs **before** vomiting/purging.|Occurs **after** vomiting/purging due to extreme dehydration.|
|**Conjunctiva**|**Inflamed and red**.|Not inflamed.|
|**Voice**|**Not affected**.|**Rough and whistling** (due to dehydration).|
|**Bacteriology**|No specific bacteria found.|_**Vibrio cholerae**_ present in stool culture.|
|**Laboratory/X-ray**|**Radiopaque shadow** on abdominal X-ray (Arsenic trioxide).|No radiopaque findings.|
|**Epidemiology**|Usually an **individual** or a specific family/group is affected.|Often occurs in **sporadic or epidemic** forms in the locality.|
|**Motive**|Often **homicidal**, rarely accidental.|No criminal motive.|

---

### **Medico-Legal Importance**

1. **Homicidal Investigation:** If a doctor certifies a death as "Cholera" when it was actually arsenic poisoning, the murderer may escape justice.
2. **Autopsy Clues:** While both show dehydration, arsenic poisoning characteristically shows a **"Red Velvety" stomach mucosa** and **subendocardial petechial hemorrhages** in the heart, which are absent in cholera.
3. **Putrefaction:** Arsenic **retards putrefaction** (delays decomposition) due to its anti-bacterial action, whereas cholera-infected bodies decompose at a normal rate.

### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Tenesmus:** The presence of tenesmus is a strong diagnostic point for arsenic over cholera.
- ★ **Order of symptoms:** In arsenic, the throat burns first; in cholera, the purging (diarrhea) starts first.
- ★ **Radiopacity:** Arsenic trioxide is radiopaque and can be seen on an X-ray, unlike cholera.

### **Viva Questions**

1. **What is the "Inheritance Powder"?**
    - _Ans:_ Arsenic trioxide ($As_2O_3$), because it was historically used for homicidal purposes to gain property.
2. **How do the stools differ between arsenic and cholera?**
    - _Ans:_ Both are rice-watery, but arsenic stools often contain blood, whereas cholera stools do not.
3. **Why does arsenic delay putrefaction?**
    - _Ans:_ It has an anti-bacterial effect and causes extreme dehydration, both of which inhibit the microorganisms responsible for decomposition.
4. **What heart finding is characteristic of acute arsenic poisoning?**
    - _Ans:_ Subendocardial petechial hemorrhages in the left ventricle.

---

### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **Arsenic:** Pain first $\rightarrow$ Vomit $\rightarrow$ Purge + Blood + Tenesmus.
- **Cholera:** Purge first $\rightarrow$ Vomit $\rightarrow$ Pain (Late) + No Blood + No Tenesmus.
- **Autopsy:** Arsenic shows "Red Velvety" stomach; Cholera shows only signs of dehydration.
- **Law:** Doctors must report suspected cases under **Section 33 BNSS**.
