### **Cocainism (Chronic Cocaine Poisoning)**

---

#### **Basic Concepts**

**Definition**  
**Cocainism**, also known as **Cocainomania** or **Cocainophagia**, is a state of chronic addiction resulting from the repeated use of cocaine, characterized by a morbid craving for the drug and progressive physical and mental deterioration.

**Introduction**  
Cocaine is a **deliriant cerebral neurotic poison** obtained from the dried leaves of the plant _**Erythroxylum coca**_. It is a colorless, odorless, crystalline substance with a bitter taste.

**Common Slang Names:**  
Snow, Coke, Crack, White Lady, Candy, and Bazooka.

**Toxicokinetics (Mechanism of Action)**

- **Hyperadrenergic State:** Cocaine blocks the reuptake of neurotransmitters (dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin), leading to their accumulation in the synaptic cleft.
- **The "Rush and Crush":** It produces an initial stimulatory effect (**"Rush"**) due to cortical stimulation, which is invariably followed by a period of depression (**"Crush"**).

---

#### **Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Aphrodisiac Myth:** It is mistakenly believed to increase libidinal drive and is used to prolong sexual acts by paralyzing sensory nerves in the genitalia.
- **Crime & Violence:** High association with binge drinking and violent crimes like homicide, assault, and robbery.
- **Body Packers/Mules:** Smugglers swallow packets of cocaine; packet rupture often leads to sudden lethal toxicity.
- **Stupefying Agent:** Occasionally used to facilitate robbery or kidnapping.

★ **Important Exam Point: Legal Responsibility**  
Under **Section 22 BNS (2023)**, involuntary intoxication provides a defense, but voluntary drunkenness is not an excuse for committing a crime.

#### **Clinical Features of Chronic Cocainism**

Chronic users show a shifty gaze and a suspicious nature due to increasing paranoia.

**I. Physical Signs**

- **Nasal Septum Perforation:** A classic sign due to the powerful vasoconstrictive action of the drug when "snorted," leading to ischaemic necrosis.
- **The Tongue and Teeth:** Characteristically become **black**.
- **Ocular Signs:** Sunken eyes and dilated pupils (**Mydriasis**).
- **General:** Emaciation, anorexia (loss of weight), and digestive disturbances.

**II. Specific Syndromes**

1. **Magnan’s Syndrome (Cocaine Bugs):** ★ **High-Yield Point**. A tactile hallucination where the addict feels as if small insects or grains of sand are crawling under the skin (**formication**). This leads to self-inflicted scratches and cuts.
2. **Cocaine Washed-out Syndrome:** A state of deep lethargy or coma following binge use, caused by dopamine depletion.
3. **Crack Lung:** A hypersensitivity pneumonitis involving chest pain and cough after smoking cocaine.

---

#### **Medico-Legal Examination & Investigations**

**I. Physical Examination**

- Search for **track marks** (injection sites) in unusual areas like under the tongue or on the feet.
- Look for "crack pipe" burns on fingers or thumbs.

**II. Laboratory Analysis**

- **Scott Test:** A presumptive test where cobalt thiocyanate produces a **blue precipitate**.
- **Mandelin Test:** The reagent produces an **orange color**.
- **Urinalysis:** Cocaine metabolites (benzoylecgonine) can be detected for **2–4 days** post-exposure.

---

#### **Management**

★ **Important Exam Point: Management follows the ABCD protocol.**

1. **Stabilization:** Maintain airway and circulation.
2. **Control Agitation/Seizures:** Intravenous **Benzodiazepines** (Diazepam) are the drug of choice.
3. **Cardiac Care:** Propranolol or Esmolol for tachycardia and hypertension.
4. **Decontamination:** Gastric lavage with **Potassium Permanganate (1:5000)** if ingested.
5. **Body Packers:** Require immediate surgical intervention if there is leakage from ingested drug packets.

---

#### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Magnan’s Syndrome:** Tactile hallucinations ("bugs under skin").
- ★ **Nasal Septum Perforation:** Result of chronic "snorting".
- ★ **Antidote:** No specific physiological antidote exists; treatment is symptomatic.
- ★ **Test:** Scott test (blue precipitate).
- ★ **Diagnosis Clue:** Black tongue and dilated pupils.

#### **Viva Questions**

1. **What is "Free-basing"?**
    - _Ans:_ Taking cocaine through the smoking route in a purified/concentrated form.
2. **How is a "Body Packer" different from a "Body Stuffer"?**
    - _Ans:_ Body packers swallow well-wrapped packets for smuggling; stuffers swallow loose drugs hurriedly to hide evidence from police.
3. **Why is Naloxone sometimes given in the "Coma Cocktail"?**
    - _Ans:_ Because many cocaine addicts use multiple drugs (speed-balling), and Naloxone reverses the opioid component.
4. **What is the "Double-condom" sign?**
    - _Ans:_ A radiological rim of air seen in X-rays of body packers between the layers of drug packaging.

---

#### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **Classification:** Deliriant Cerebral Poison.
- **Active Principle:** Alkaloid from _Erythroxylum coca_.
- **Chronic Signs:** Nasal perforation, black tongue, emaciation.
- **Hallucination:** Cocaine Bugs (Magnan’s Syndrome).
- **Legal:** Mandatory reporting under Section 33 BNSS.
- **Treatment:** Supportive + Diazepam for seizures.
