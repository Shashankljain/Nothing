### **Classification of Poisons and Organophosphate Poisoning**
[[Toxicology]]
---

### **Part I: Classification of Poisons**

Poisons are generally classified based on their **mode of action** on the human body.

|**Class**|**Description**|**Examples**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**1. Corrosives**|Cause chemical destruction and ulceration of tissues on contact.|**Acids:** Sulphuric, Nitric. **Alkalis:** Caustic soda.|
|**2. Irritants**|Cause inflammation, irritation, or ulceration; symptoms appear slowly.|**Inorganic:** Arsenic, Lead, Mercury, Phosphorus. **Organic:** Castor, Snake venom, Scorpions. **Mechanical:** Powdered glass, needles.|
|**3. Neurotics**|Act primarily on the Central Nervous System (CNS).|**Cerebral:** Alcohol (Inebriant), Opium (Somniferous), Dhatura (Deliriant). **Spinal:** Nux vomica (Strychnine). **Peripheral:** Conium.|
|**4. Cardiac**|Act directly on the heart or its nerve supply.|Digitalis, Oleander, Aconite, Nicotine.|
|**5. Asphyxiants**|Deprive the body of oxygen.|Carbon monoxide (CO), Cyanide (HCN).|
|**6. Miscellaneous**|Includes various agrochemicals and drugs of dependence.|**Organophosphates**, Kerosene, Paracetamol.|

### **Part II: Organophosphate (OP) Poisoning**

#### **1. Basic Concepts**

**Introduction**  
Organophosphates are a group of highly toxic agrochemicals widely used as **pesticides and insecticides** in India (e.g., Malathion, Parathion, Diazinon). Due to their easy availability in agricultural areas, they are a common cause of poisoning.

**Mechanism of Action (Toxicodynamics)**  
★ **Important Exam Point: Acetylcholinesterase Inhibition**

1. OP compounds bind to and inhibit the enzyme **Acetylcholinesterase (AChE)**.
2. Normally, AChE breaks down the neurotransmitter **Acetylcholine (ACh)** at the nerve synapse.
3. Inhibition of AChE leads to an **accumulation of ACh** at the synaptic cleft, resulting in continuous and over-stimulation of the nervous system.

---

#### **2. Clinical Features (Manifestations)**

OP poisoning produces a **Cholinergic Toxidrome**.

**I. Muscarinic Effects (Mnemonic: SLUDGE)**

- **S:** Salivation (Excessive)
- **L:** Lacrimation (Tearing)
- **U:** Urination
- **D:** Defecation (Diarrhea)
- **G:** Gastrointestinal motility (Cramps/Vomiting)
- **E:** Emesis

**II. Nicotinic Effects**

- Muscle fasciculations (twitching), weakness, and eventually respiratory muscle **paralysis**.

**III. CNS Effects**

- Seizures, coma, and respiratory depression.
- ★ **Key Sign:** **Pinpoint pupils (Miosis)**.
- ★ **Characteristic Odour:** A strong **kerosene-like smell** in the breath and vomitus (due to the solvent used).

---

#### **3. Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Suicide:** Most common manner in India due to cheap agricultural availability.
- **Accidental:** Farmers inhaling spray or children ingesting stored containers.
- **Homicide:** Rare due to the strong kerosene odour which is difficult to mask.
- ★ **Occupational Hazard:** Chronic exposure in workers can lead to polyneuropathy.

---

#### **4. Relevant Law (2023 Provisions)**

|**Act**|**Section**|**Medico-Legal Significance**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**BNSS**|**33**|**Mandatory Reporting:** Doctors must inform the police of any suspected poisoning case.|
|**BNS**|**123**|**Hurt by Poison:** Administering OP with intent to cause hurt or facilitate a crime.|
|**BNS**|**101**|**Punishment for Murder:** If poisoning results in death.|
|**BNS**|**211**|**Non-compliance:** Punishment for doctors failing to report the case.|

---

#### **5. Management**

Management follows the **ABCD** mnemonic (Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Drugs).

1. **Decontamination:**
    - **Skin:** Remove contaminated clothes and wash skin with soap and water.
    - **Gastric Lavage:** Perform within 1–2 hours of ingestion using **Potassium Permanganate (1:5000)** to oxidize the poison.
2. **Specific Antidotes:**
    - **Atropine:** The **physiological antagonist**. It blocks muscarinic receptors to stop secretions and bradycardia. (End-point: Drying of secretions and dilated pupils).
    - **Pralidoxime (Oximes/PAM):** The **enzyme reactivator**. It "breaks" the bond between OP and AChE if given before "aging" occurs.
3. **Supportive Care:** Maintain airway; mechanical ventilation may be required if respiratory muscles are paralyzed.

---

#### **6. Postmortem Findings (Autopsy)**

- **External:** **Kerosene-like odour** from the mouth; cyanosis; miosis (contracted pupils).
- **Internal:**
    - **Stomach:** Mucosa is congested; contains a kerosene-smelling liquid.
    - **Lungs:** Heavy, edematous, and congested (froth in air passages).
    - **General:** Congestion of all internal organs.

---

#### **7. Diagnosis and Investigations**

- **Clinical Diagnosis:** Based on **miosis**, **kerosene odour**, and **SLUDGE** symptoms.
- **Laboratory:** Estimation of **Plasma Cholinesterase** levels (it will be significantly reduced).
- **Viscera Preservation:** Preserved in **Saturated Common Salt** for chemical analysis.

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Antidote Triad:** Atropine (antagonist) + PAM (reactivator) + Diazepam (for seizures).
- ★ **Odour:** Kerosene (pathognomonic clue in university questions).
- ★ **Miosis:** Pinpoint pupils are the hallmark of OP, Opium, and Phenol poisoning.

---

### **Quick Revision Summary**

1. **Nature:** Agrochemical / Irritant.
2. **Mechanism:** Inhibits Acetylcholinesterase $\rightarrow$ Acetylcholine accumulation.
3. **Signs:** SLUDGE, Miosis, Kerosene smell.
4. **Treatment:** Atropine (until secretions dry) + PAM.
5. **Law:** Mandatory reporting under Section 33 BNSS.
6. **Autopsy:** Kerosene smell and pulmonary edema.
