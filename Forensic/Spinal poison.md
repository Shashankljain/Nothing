### **Strychnine Poisoning (Nux Vomica)**

---

#### **1. Basic Concepts**

**Introduction**  
Strychnine is one of the oldest known poisons, obtained from the seeds of the evergreen tree **Strychnos nux-vomica**(Family: Loganiaceae). It is classified as a **Spinal Neurotic Poison**.

**What? (Common Names):**  
Nux vomica, poison nut, Quaker buttons, _kuchila_, and _yetti_.

**Active Principles:**

- **Strychnine** (Main alkaloid).
- **Brucine** (Alkaloid).
- **Loganin** (Glucoside).

**Toxicodynamics (Mechanism of Action):**  
★ **Important Exam Point:** Strychnine is a competitive antagonist of **glycine**, which is an inhibitory neurotransmitter.

- **How:** It blocks glycine receptors in the anterior horn cells of the spinal cord (specifically **Renshaw cells**).
- **Effect:** By blocking inhibition, it causes "release excitation," leading to constant, powerful muscle contractions.

---

#### **2. Clinical Features (Signs and Symptoms)**

Symptoms typically appear within **15–30 minutes** of ingestion.

**I. Prodromal (Early) Symptoms**

- Bitter taste in the mouth.
- A "choking sensation" in the throat and stiffness of the face and neck.
- Increased acuity of perception (hyperesthesia) to light, sound, and touch.

**II. The "Conscious" Seizure ★**  
The hallmark of strychnine is that the patient remains **fully awake and aware** during the convulsions.

1. **Convulsions:** Initially clonic (intermittent), then becoming tonic (continuous). They affect all muscles simultaneously.
2. **Trigger:** Even a slight stimulus (a gust of air, a light touch, or a sudden noise) can trigger a fresh convulsion.
3. **Risus Sardonicus (Scornful Grin):** Facial muscles contract, pulling the corners of the mouth back and raising the eyebrows into an "evil" expression.
4. **Opisthotonus:** The body is arched backwards so that only the head and heels touch the ground.
    - _Note:_ If the body bends forward, it is **Emprosthotonus**; if sideways, it is **Pleurosthotonus**.
5. **Respiratory Distress:** The chest and diaphragm are fixed during a spasm, making breathing impossible and causing cyanosis.
6. **Resolution:** In between convulsions, the muscles relax completely, but the patient remains in exhaustion and fear.

#### **4. Management (Treatment)**

★ **Important Exam Point: The priority is to stop the convulsions and provide a quiet environment.**

1. **Immediate Stabilization (ABCD):**
    - **Airway:** Maintain a clear airway; endotracheal intubation may be needed.
    - **Breathing:** Oxygen and mechanical ventilation if respiratory muscles are paralyzed.
2. **Specific Antidotes:**
    - **Barbiturates:** (Sodium Amytal or Pentobarbital) are the physiological antidotes (Dose: 300–600 mg IV).
    - **Benzodiazepines:** (Diazepam 0.1–0.5 mg/kg IV) to control muscle spasms.
3. **Decontamination:**
    - **Gastric Lavage:** Performed with **1:5000 Potassium Permanganate ($KMnO_4$)** to oxidize the alkaloids.
    - **CRITICAL CAUTION:** Lavage should only be done **after** the patient is sedated and the airway is secured, as the tube itself can trigger a fatal convulsion.
    - **Activated Charcoal:** Given to adsorb the poison.
4. **Environment:** Keep the patient in a **dark, quiet room**, free from all noise and disturbance.
5. **Hyperthermia Control:** Active cooling (ice water, cooling blankets) for high fever caused by muscle activity.

---

#### **5. Medico-Legal Importance & Autopsy**

- **Fatal Dose:** 15–50 mg or **one crushed seed**.
- **Fatal Period:** 1–2 hours.
- **Accidental:** Most common; often from overdose of medicinal tonics or children eating the seeds.
- **Homicide:** Rare because of the extreme bitterness.
- **Autopsy Finding:** Signs of asphyxia and **early onset of Rigor Mortis**. **Cadaveric spasm** is often present as the body "freezes" in the position of the last convulsion.

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Risus Sardonicus:** The "evil grin" of strychnine/tetanus.
- ★ **Opisthotonus:** Backwards arching of the body.
- ★ **Glycine:** The specific neurotransmitter blocked.
- ★ **Conscious Seizure:** Key differentiator from epilepsy.
- ★ **Antidote:** Barbiturates (Sodium Amytal).

### **Comparison: Strychnine vs. Tetanus**

_Examiners frequently ask this in Viva/Long answers._

|**Feature**|**Strychnine Poisoning**|**Tetanus**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Onset**|Sudden (within minutes/hours).|Gradual (several days).|
|**Trismus (Lockjaw)**|Starts late or at the same time.|**Starts first** and affects lower jaw.|
|**Muscular State**|**Relaxed** between convulsions.|**Rigid** throughout.|
|**Fatal Period**|1–2 hours.|> 24 hours.|
|**Chemical Analysis**|Strychnine found in viscera.|No poison found; culture shows _C. tetani_.|

### **Quick Revision Summary**

1. **Nature:** Spinal Neurotic / _S. nux-vomica_.
2. **Action:** Glycine Antagonist (Renshaw cells).
3. **Classic Signs:** Opisthotonus + Risus Sardonicus + Conscious mind.
4. **Treatment:** Dark quiet room + Barbiturates/Diazepam + Lavage (with secured airway).
5. **Law:** Section 123 BNS and Section 33 BNSS.
