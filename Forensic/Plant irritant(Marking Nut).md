[[Organic irritants]]
# **Marking Nut (_Semecarpus anacardium_)**

### **Basic Concepts**

**Introduction**

- **Definition:** The marking nut is a plant irritant poison derived from the fruit of the tree _**Semecarpus anacardium**_.
- **Common Names:** Known as **Bhilawa**, **Ballataka**, or 'Oriental Cashew Nut'.
- **Origin of Name:** It is called the "marking nut" because its juice is used by washermen (dhobis) as **indelible ink** to mark clothes with identification numbers.

**Identification of Seeds**

- The seeds are black, cone-shaped, or heart-shaped with a rough projection at the base.
- They have a thick pericarp (outer shell) containing an **oily, acrid juice** that is brownish when fresh but turns black on exposure to air.

**Active Principles** The irritant property is due to phenolic compounds:

1. **Bhilawanol** (a mixture of isomers of urushiol).
2. **Anacardic acid**.
3. **Semecarpol** and **Cardol**.

---

### **Clinical Features (Signs and Symptoms)**

**1. Skin Contact (External)**

- Initial irritation, itching, and a **burning sensation**.
- Formation of **painful blisters** containing acrid (irritating) serum.
- The skin lesion often **resembles a bruise**, which is a significant medico-legal finding.
- The rash typically takes 1–2 weeks to heal and rarely leaves permanent scars.

**2. Ingestion (Internal)**

- **Oral:** Blisters in the mouth and throat.
- **Gastrointestinal:** Severe GIT irritation, nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.
- **Systemic:** Tachycardia, hypotension, cyanosis, loss of reflexes, delirium, and coma.

**Fatal Dose and Period**

- **Fatal Dose:** 5–10 grams or approximately **5–8 seeds**.
- **Fatal Period:** **12–24 hours**.

---

### **Medico-Legal Importance**

★ **Important Exam Point: Artificial Bruise** The most common medico-legal use of the marking nut is the creation of **artificial bruises** to support false charges of assault or to escape military/police duty.

- **Abortifacient:** The juice is applied to an "abortion stick" and introduced into the cervical canal to procure criminal abortion.
- **Vitriolage:** The juice may be thrown on a person's face to cause injury or disfigurement, similar to acid throwing.
- **Punishment:** Historically used in some cultures to be introduced into the vagina as a punishment for infidelity.
- **Malingering:** Used to produce conjunctivitis or skin lesions to feign illness.

### **Management**

1. **Skin Exposure:** Wash the contaminated area immediately with **soap and water**.
2. **Oral Intake:**
    - **Gastric Lavage:** Performed carefully if ingested.
    - **Demulcents:** Administer milk or egg white to soothe the mucosa.
    - **Symptomatic Treatment:** IV fluids for shock and pain management.

---

### **Autopsy Findings**

- **External:** Presence of **bruise-like lesions** with small blisters near the mouth or on accessible body parts.
- **Internal:**
    - **Stomach:** Mucosa is congested, inflamed, and may show erosions.
    - **Liver:** May show degenerative changes.
    - **Lungs:** Features of asphyxia (congestion and edema).

---

### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Indelible Ink:** Marking nut juice is used for marking clothes.
- ★ **Bhilawanol:** The primary active principle.
- ★ **Artificial Bruise:** Key differentiator is the **presence of itching and blisters**.
- ★ **Grievous Hurt:** Disfigurement via vitriolage falls under **BNS Section 116**.

### **Quick Revision Summary**

1. **Nature:** Organic plant irritant.
2. **Active Principle:** Bhilawanol.
3. **Key Feature:** Used to create artificial bruises.
4. **Differentiating Sign:** Artificial bruises itch; true bruises do not.
5. **Antidote:** None; treat with soap, water, and demulcents.
