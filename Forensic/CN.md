[[Asphyxiants]]
### **Cyanide Poisoning**

---

#### **Basic Concepts**

**Definition**  
**Cyanide poisoning** is a form of rapid systemic toxicity caused by exposure to hydrocyanic acid (HCN) or its salts (e.g., Potassium Cyanide, Sodium Cyanide). It is classically categorized as an **Asphyxiant Poison** because it deprives the body of oxygen at the cellular level.

**Introduction**  
Cyanide is one of the most rapidly acting and lethal poisons known. It is often referred to as the "ideal suicidal poison" due to its high toxicity, ease of use (in certain forms), and the painless, extremely rapid death it produces.

**Common Forms:**

- **Hydrocyanic acid (HCN):** A volatile liquid or gas with a characteristic odor.
- **Potassium/Sodium Cyanide (KCN/NaCN):** White, crystalline salts used in industries like electroplating and gold mining.

---

#### **Mechanism of Action (Toxicodynamics)**

★ **Important Exam Point: Histotoxic Anoxia**

1. **Inhibition of Cellular Respiration:** Cyanide enters the cells and binds with high affinity to the **ferric ($Fe^{3+}$) iron** in the **cytochrome oxidase enzyme** system within the mitochondria.
2. **Blockage of Electron Transport Chain:** By binding to this enzyme, cyanide prevents the final step of the electron transport chain, stopping the cell from using oxygen to produce ATP.
3. **Histotoxic Anoxia:** Although the blood is fully saturated with oxygen (arterial $O_2$ is normal), the tissues cannot utilize it. This state where oxidative processes are inhibited in the tissue is known as **Histotoxic Anoxia**.
4. **Lactic Acidosis:** Cells shift to anaerobic metabolism, leading to a massive buildup of lactic acid and a high anion-gap metabolic acidosis.

---

#### **Clinical Features (Signs and Symptoms)**

Symptoms appear almost instantaneously after inhalation or within minutes of ingestion.

**I. Physical Signs**

- **Odor:** A characteristic **"Bitter Almond" smell** in the breath or vomitus (detectable by about 60–80% of the population).
- **Color:** The skin and mucous membranes may appear **bright red or pink** because the oxygenated hemoglobin is not being used by the tissues.
- **Ocular:** **Mydriasis** (dilated pupils).

**II. Symptoms (Toxidrome)**

1. **Initial:** Headache, dizziness, palpitations, and a feeling of constriction in the throat.
2. **Progressive:** Dyspnea (breathing difficulty), gasping for air, and hypertension followed by hypotension.
3. **Terminal:** Generalized convulsions, deep coma, and death within minutes due to respiratory center paralysis.

#### **Management (Treatment)**

Management must be immediate and follows the **ABCD** protocol.

**I. Stabilization**

- **Airway/Breathing:** Administer **100% Oxygen** to maximize the amount of dissolved $O_2$ in the plasma.

**II. Antidote Therapy (Lilly's Cyanide Antidote Kit) ★** The goal is to provide alternative binding sites for cyanide to pull it away from the mitochondria.

1. **Amyl Nitrite (Inhalation) & Sodium Nitrite (IV):** These convert hemoglobin to **Methemoglobin**. Cyanide binds to methemoglobin to form **Cyanmethemoglobin**, which is relatively non-toxic.
2. **Sodium Thiosulphate (IV):** This facilitates the enzymatic conversion of cyanide to **Thiocyanate** (via the enzyme rhodanase), which is safely excreted in the urine.
3. **Hydroxocobalamin (Vitamin B12a):** Considered the **best and safest antidote**; it binds cyanide to form cyanocobalamin (Vitamin B12).

---

#### **Postmortem Appearance (Autopsy Findings)**

**External Findings:**

- **Odor:** Distinctive **smell of bitter almonds** on opening the body cavities.
- **Postmortem Staining:** Characteristically **Bright Red, Brick Red, or Pink**.
- **Pupils:** Usually dilated.

**Internal Findings:**

- **Stomach:** The mucosa is often congested and may show "corrosion" if KCN (which is alkaline) was ingested.
- **Viscera:** General congestion of all organs.
- **Blood:** Fluid, bright red, and remains oxygenated.
- **Specimen Collection:** The **Spleen** is a vital organ to preserve as it concentrates toxins like cyanide that bind to hemoglobin.

---

#### **Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Suicide:** Extremely common agent for suicide among goldsmiths, photographers, and scientists.
- **Homicide:** Rare due to the rapid onset and characteristic odor, making it difficult to administer secretly.
- **Accidental:** Occupational exposure in industries or during fires (cyanide gas is released from burning plastics/silk).
- ★ **Important Exam Point:** It is an **Ideal Suicidal Poison** because it is cheap, highly toxic, tasteless, and results in a rapid, painless death.

---

#### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Type of Anoxia:** Histotoxic Anoxia.
- ★ **Specific Odor:** Bitter Almonds.
- ★ **PM Staining Color:** Bright Red / Pink.
- ★ **Antidote Strategy:** Nitrites (to form Methemoglobin) + Thiosulphate (to form Thiocyanate).
- ★ **Best Antidote:** Hydroxocobalamin.

#### **Viva Questions**

1. **Why is the PM staining red in cyanide poisoning despite it being an "asphyxiant"?**
    - _Ans:_ Because the tissues cannot take up the oxygen from the blood; hence the venous blood remains highly oxygenated and appears bright red.
2. **Which enzyme does cyanide inhibit?**
    - _Ans:_ Cytochrome oxidase (specifically the $a_3$ component).
3. **What is the role of Sodium Thiosulphate?**
    - _Ans:_ It acts as a sulfur donor to the enzyme rhodanase to convert cyanide into non-toxic thiocyanate.
4. **Under which section of BNSS must you report this case?**
    - _Ans:_ Section 33 of the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023.

#### **Quick Revision Summary**

1. **Mechanism:** Histotoxic anoxia via Cytochrome Oxidase inhibition.
2. **Diagnosis:** Bitter almond smell + Bright red PM staining.
3. **Antidote:** Amyl/Sodium Nitrite + Sodium Thiosulphate (or Hydroxocobalamin).
4. **Legal:** Section 33 BNSS (Mandatory reporting).
