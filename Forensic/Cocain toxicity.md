### **Cocaine Toxicity (Acute and Chronic)**
[[Deliriants]]
---

#### **Basic Concepts**

**Definition**  
Cocaine is an **alkaloid deliriant** obtained from the dried leaves of the plant _**Erythroxylum coca**_. It acts as a potent Central Nervous System (CNS) stimulant.

**Mechanism of Action**

- **Hyperadrenergic State:** Cocaine blocks the reuptake of neurotransmitters like dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin, leading to their accumulation in the synaptic cleft.
- **The "Rush and Crush":** It causes an initial stimulatory effect (**"Rush"**) due to cortical stimulation, which is invariably followed by a period of depression (**"Crush"**).
- **Local Anaesthetic:** It blocks nerve conduction by decreasing axonal membrane permeability to sodium ions.

---

#### **Clinical Features (Signs and Symptoms)**

Cocaine toxicity is typically described in two stages: the **Stage of Excitement (Rush)** and the **Stage of Depression (Crush)**.

##### **I. Stage of Excitement (Acute "Rush")**

Symptoms appear rapidly (within seconds if inhaled or IV).

- **Vital Signs:** Elevated pulse, blood pressure, respiration, and temperature (hyperthermia).
- **CNS:** Euphoria, talkativeness, restlessness, delirium, maniacal behavior, and hallucinations.
- **Physical Signs:** **Mydriasis** (dilated pupils) leading to blurred vision, and a flushed face.
- **Muscular:** Non-intentional tremors (facial/fingers), bruxism (teeth grinding), and exaggerated reflexes.
- **GIT:** Bitter taste, dryness of mouth, vomiting, and diarrhea.

##### **II. Stage of Depression (Acute "Crush")**

Usually occurs about an hour after the initial rush.

- **CNS:** Coma, **areflexia** (loss of reflexes), and flaccid paralysis.
- **Ocular:** Pupils become fixed and dilated.
- **CVS:** Irregular pulse, hypotension, ventricular dysrhythmias, and potentially cardiac arrest.
- **RS:** **Cheyne-Stokes respiration**, cyanosis, and respiratory failure.

##### **III. Chronic Toxicity (Cocainism/Cocainomania)**

Results from repeated use and leads to physical and mental deterioration.

- **Magnan’s Syndrome (Cocaine Bugs):** ★ **Important Exam Point**. A tactile hallucination where the user feels as if insects or grains of sand are crawling under the skin (**formication**).
- **Nasal Septum Perforation:** A classic sign in those who "snort" cocaine due to chronic vasoconstriction leading to tissue death.
- **Physical Findings:** **Black tongue** and teeth, sunken eyes, and extreme emaciation (weight loss).
- **Systemic Effects:** Anorexia, loss of libido, and major menstrual derangements (amenorrhea).

---

#### **Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Crime:** High association with violent crimes and robbery.
- **Body Packers:** Smugglers swallow packets of cocaine; if these rupture, it leads to sudden lethal toxicity.
- **Aphrodisiac Myth:** Mistakenly used to increase sexual drive, though it eventually causes dysfunction.

#### **Investigations and Diagnosis**

1. **Scott Test (Presumptive):** Cobalt thiocyanate produces a **blue precipitate**.
2. **Mandelin Test:** Reagent produces an **orange color**.
3. **Urinalysis:** Can detect metabolites (benzoylecgonine) for **2–4 days** after use.
4. **Radiology:** "Double-condom" sign (air between packaging layers) in body packers.

---

#### **Management**

★ **Important Exam Point: Management follows the ABCD (Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Drugs) protocol.**

- **Stabilisation:** Maintain airway and circulation; use oxygen or mechanical ventilation if needed.
- **Control Seizures:** Intravenous **Benzodiazepines** (Diazepam) are the drug of choice.
- **Cardiac Care:** Propranolol or Esmolol for severe tachycardia and hypertension.
- **Decontamination:** Gastric lavage with **KMnO₄ (1:5000)** if ingested.

---

#### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Magnan’s Syndrome:** Tactile hallucinations ("bugs under skin").
- ★ **Nasal Septum Perforation:** Specific sign of chronic snorting.
- ★ **Scott Test:** Diagnostic blue precipitate.
- ★ **Rush and Crush:** The classic two-stage acute presentation.

#### **Viva Questions**

1. **What are "Cocaine Bugs"?**  
    _Ans:_ It is Magnan’s Syndrome, a tactile hallucination of insects crawling under the skin seen in chronic addicts.
2. **What is the fatal dose of cocaine?**  
    _Ans:_ Approximately 20 mg IV or 500 mg to 1.2 g orally.
3. **Why do users develop a black tongue?**  
    _Ans:_ This is a common physical sign of chronic cocaine addiction (cocainism).
4. **What is "Free-basing"?**  
    _Ans:_ A method of smoking purified cocaine to achieve a faster high.
