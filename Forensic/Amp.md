### **Methyl Alcohol (Methanol) Poisoning**

---

#### **1. Basic Concepts**

**Introduction**  
**Methyl Alcohol (Methanol)**, also known as **Wood Alcohol, Wood Naphtha, or Wood Spirits**, is a colorless, volatile liquid with a burning taste and an odor similar to ethyl alcohol. It is widely used as an **==industrial solvent==** and is a frequent constituent of **==antifreeze==**, duplicating fluids, and cleaning materials.

**Toxicokinetics (Metabolism)**

- **Absorption:** It is rapidly absorbed through the gastrointestinal tract (GIT), lungs (inhalation), and skin.
- **Pathophysiology:** Methanol is a **==cumulative poison==** because its oxidation occurs at only 15% the rate of ethyl alcohol.
- **Metabolism:** It is converted in the liver by the enzyme **alcohol dehydrogenase** into **Formaldehyde**, which is then converted into **Formic Acid**.
- **Mechanism of Toxicity:** **Formic acid** is the toxic metabolite responsible for causing a high anion-gap metabolic acidosis and specific damage to the ==*retina and optic nerve*==.

**Fatal Dose and Period**

- **Fatal Dose:** **60–140 mL** (blood levels >150 mg/dL are usually fatal).
- **Fatal Period:** **24–36 hours**.

---

#### **2. Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Hooch Tragedies:** Most cases are **accidental**, resulting from the consumption of adulterated illicit liquor where methanol is used as a cheap substitute for ethanol.
- **Suicide/Homicide:** These are rare but can occur.
- **Occupational Hazard:** Risk exists for workers in industries using methanol as a solvent.

★ **Important Exam Point: Mandatory Reporting**  
Under **Section 33 BNSS (2023)**, it is a doctor's duty to inform the police of any suspected poisoning case attended in the hospital.

#### **4. Clinical Features (Symptoms and Signs)**

Symptoms typically appear after a latent period of **12–24 hours**.

**I. Gastrointestinal (GIT)**

- Nausea, vomiting, and severe abdominal cramps.
- A *==spirit-like odor==* may be perceived in the breath.

**II. Ocular (Hallmark Feature) ★**

- **"Snowfield Vision"**: Misty or blurred vision, as if looking through a snowstorm.
- 'Halo vision' (bright circles around lights).
- **Signs:** Pupils are fixed and **dilated (mydriasis)**; optic neuritis and atrophy can lead to permanent blindness.

**III. Central Nervous System (CNS)**

- Headache, dizziness, vertigo, restlessness, and delirium.
- Severe cases lead to coma and convulsions.

**IV. Systemic/Renal**

- **High Anion-Gap Metabolic Acidosis:** Characterized by rapid, deep breathing (Kussmaul breathing).
- Scanty urine output.

---

#### **5. Management**

**I. Immediate Stabilization**

- Maintain the airway and support vitals using the **ABCD** protocol (Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Drugs).

**II. Gastric Decontamination**

- **Gastric Lavage:** Performed with **5% Sodium Bicarbonate** solution to inhibit the absorption of any remaining methanol.

**III. Combating Acidosis ★**

- Administration of **IV Sodium Bicarbonate** (2 g in 250 mL of water every 4 hours) is the most critical step to correct metabolic acidosis.

**IV. Specific Antidotes**

1. **Ethanol Therapy:** Administered as a competitive antagonist. Ethanol has a much higher affinity for alcohol dehydrogenase than methanol, thus preventing the formation of toxic formic acid.
    - Desired blood ethanol level: **100–150 mg/dL**.
2. **Fomepizole (4-methylpyrazole):** A potent competitive inhibitor of alcohol dehydrogenase; it is the antidote of choice as it blocks the formation of toxic metabolites without causing CNS depression.

**V. Supportive and Enhanced Elimination**

- **Folate Therapy:** Calcium folinate (Leucovorin) IV is given to enhance the metabolism and breakdown of formic acid.
- **Hemodialysis:** Highly effective and indicated in severe cases to remove both methanol and formic acid from the blood.
- **Eye Protection:** Eyes should be kept covered to protect them from light.

---

#### **6. Autopsy Findings (Internal & External)**

- **External:** Marked cyanosis (bluish discoloration) and prominent **purple** postmortem staining.
- **Stomach:** Mucosa is congested and inflamed, often showing small hemorrhages.
- **Brain:**
    - Diffuse cerebral edema.
    - **Symmetrical Infarction/Necrosis:** Specifically affects the **putamen** and sub-cortical white matter (a diagnostic finding).
- **Lungs:** Heavily congested and edematous.
- **Odor:** All viscera may smell of alcohol.

---

#### **7. High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Snowfield Vision:** Pathognomonic visual disturbance.
- ★ **Formic Acid:** The metabolite responsible for acidosis and blindness.
- ★ **Putamen Necrosis:** Characteristic brain finding at autopsy.
- ★ **Ethanol/Fomepizole:** The primary specific antidotes.
- ★ **PAPPA Mnemonic:** Rectified spirit is contraindicated as a preservative for viscera in alcohol (Ethanol/Methanol) poisoning; use **Saturated Common Salt** instead.

---

#### **Quick Revision Summary**

1. **Identity:** Industrial wood alcohol; common adulterant in "Hooch."
2. **Metabolism:** Methanol $\rightarrow$ Formaldehyde $\rightarrow$ **Formic Acid** (Toxic).
3. **Signs:** High anion-gap acidosis, **Snowfield vision**, and Dilated pupils.
4. **Antidotes:** **Sodium Bicarbonate** (for acidosis) + **Ethanol** or **Fomepizole**.
5. **Autopsy:** **Putamen necrosis** and purple postmortem staining.
6. **Law:** Mandatory reporting under **Section 33 BNSS**.
