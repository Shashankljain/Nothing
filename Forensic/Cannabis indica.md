[[Deliriants]]

---

#### **Basic Concepts**

**Definition**  
**Cannabis indica** (Indian variety) is a **deliriant cerebral neurotic** hemp plant. It is one of the most commonly abused substances worldwide after nicotine, alcohol, and caffeine.

**Introduction**  
The plant _Cannabis sativa_ has several varieties, including _C. indica_ (India), _C. mexicana_ (Mexico), and _C. americana_(US). In India, it grows as a wild plant in many regions, and the whole plant is considered poisonous.

**Common Names:** Pot, grass, weed, dope, joint, Mary Jane, hash, reefer, or ganja.

**Active Principle**

- Cannabis does **not** contain alkaloids.
- The active principle is a fat-soluble oleoresin called **cannabinol**.
- The most active form is **$\delta$-9-tetrahydrocannabinol (THC)**.
- It also contains **benzopyrene**, a known carcinogen also found in tobacco.

**Classification of Preparations (★ Important Exam Point)**  
The toxicity and effects depend on which part of the plant is used:

|**Preparation**|**Source**|**THC Content**|**Form/Use**|
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
|**Bhang (Siddhi/Patti)**|Dried leaves and shoots.|2–5% (Least potent).|Ground to a paste; used as a beverage or in sweets (Majum).|
|**Ganja**|Flowering tops of female plant.|5–8%.|Rusty green color; mixed with tobacco and smoked.|
|**Charas (Hashish)**|Resinous exudate from leaves/flowers/stems.|10–20% (Most potent).|Dark green/brown; smoked in pipes/hukka.|
|**Hash Oil**|Lipid-soluble plant extract.|25–50%.|Added to hashish or marijuana to increase potency.|

#### **Toxicokinetics and Toxicodynamics**

- **Mechanism of Action:** THC binds to **anandamide receptors** in the brain. It acts as a stimulant, sedative, or hallucinogen depending on the dose.
- **Absorption:** Rapidly absorbed via inhalation (smoking/“chasing the dragon”). Symptoms appear within minutes of smoking and within 30 minutes of oral ingestion.
- **Metabolism:** Metabolized in the liver; can be detected in urine for up to 7 days after exposure.

---

#### **Clinical Features (Signs and Symptoms)**

Toxicity manifests in two primary stages:

**I. Stage of Excitement**

- **Behavioral:** Euphoria, talkativeness, laughter, and an exaggerated sense of well-being.
- **Cognitive:** Subjective sense of time slowing down; impairment of short-term memory and judgment.
- **Physical:** **Conjunctival injection** (bloodshot eyes), **mydriasis** (dilated pupils), increased appetite (known as **"the munchies"**), and tachycardia.

**II. Stage of Narcosis**

- Giddiness, confusion, and ataxia.
- The patient passes into a deep sleep and typically wakes up **without a hangover**.

**Special Conditions:**

1. **Run-amok (★ Important Exam Point):** A psychic disturbance caused by sudden or continued consumption. It is characterized by **homicidal mania** (an uncontrollable impulse to murder). The addict may kill several people before attempting suicide or surrendering. There is usually **no recollection** of the event afterward.
2. **Cannabinoid Hyperemesis Syndrome (CHS):** Results from chronic use. Features cyclic episodes of nausea and vomiting, often relieved by **compulsive hot bathing**.

---

#### **Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Criminal Responsibility:** Under Indian law, a person in a state of **'run amok'** is often considered to have a disorder of the mind rather than simple intoxication, provided they did not take the drug purposefully to commit the crime.
- **Stupefying Agent:** Charas and Majum are used by thieves to stupefy victims for robbery.
- **Accidental Poisoning:** Common in children who ingest cannabis-infused sweets or "chocolates".

#### **Management**

1. **Gastric Decontamination:** Gastric lavage with warm water or administration of activated charcoal.
2. **Supportive Care:** IV fluids (50% glucose/dextrose) and placement in a quiet, dark room.
3. **Sedation:** **Diazepam** (5–10 mg IV) if the patient is violent or aggressive.
4. **Psychotic Symptoms:** **Haloperidol** is used to control hallucinations or acute CHS.

---

#### **Autopsy Findings**

- **External:** Non-specific; signs of asphyxia (cyanosis and congestion).
- **Odor:** A characteristic **"burnt rope"** smell may be perceived.
- **Internal:** Congestion of all visceral organs and the brain.

### **Viva Questions**

1. **What is the "Munchies"?**
    - _Ans:_ An intense increase in appetite following cannabis use.
2. **How is Ganja different from Charas?**
    - _Ans:_ Ganja comes from the flowering tops (5–8% THC), while Charas is the resinous exudate (10–20% THC).
3. **What is the legal limit for 'Small Quantity' of cannabis?**
    - _Ans:_ Under the NDPS Act, small quantity is 100g for cannabis and 1,000g for ganja.
4. **Is there an antidote for Cannabis?**
    - _Ans:_ No specific antidote exists; treatment is primarily supportive.
