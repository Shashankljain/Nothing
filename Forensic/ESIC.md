### **Employees' State Insurance (ESI) Act, 1948**

---

#### **Basic Concepts**

**Introduction** The **Employees' State Insurance (ESI) Act, 1948**, is a pivotal social security legislation in India. It is designed to provide certain benefits to employees in case of sickness, maternity, and 'employment injury' and to make provision for certain other matters in relation thereto.

**Objectives**

- To provide medical care and cash benefits to employees.
- To ensure financial security for workers and their dependents during periods of physical distress.
- To mandate the maintenance of accurate medical records for the purpose of verifying claims.

**Importance in Medico-Legal Practice** A Registered Medical Practitioner (RMP) plays a crucial role in the implementation of this Act by:

1. **Issuing Certificates:** Providing "Sickness" and "Fitness" certificates which serve as primary evidence for claiming benefits.
2. **Documentary Evidence:** Medical records act as essential documentary evidence in courts for labor-related legal cases.
3. **Disability Assessment:** Assessing the degree of injury or illness to determine the compensation amount.

---

#### **Medico-Legal Importance**

★ **Important Exam Point: Role in Claims**

- **Civil Cases:** Documentation is vital for "Workman’s Compensation Act claims" and ESI benefits to prove the extent of disability or illness.
- **Expert Opinion:** Doctors may be summoned as **Expert Witnesses (Section 39 BSA)** to interpret medical facts regarding an employee's health status.
- **Exhumation:** In rare civil disputes regarding inheritance or workmen's compensation, a body may be exhumed to establish the cause of death.

---

#### **Relevant Law (BNS & BNSS, 2023)**

Under the new criminal codes, the issuance and handling of medical certificates for ESI/Labor claims are strictly regulated:

|**Act**|**Section**|**Provision**|**Medico-Legal Significance**|
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
|**BNS**|**234**|False Certificate|Punishment for issuing or signing a false medical certificate.|
|**BNS**|**227**|Perjury|Giving false evidence under oath during a claim hearing.|
|**BNS**|**228**|Fabricating Evidence|Creating false medical records to facilitate a fraudulent claim.|
|**BNSS**|**33**|Information to Police|Mandatory for doctors to report if an 'employment injury' suggests a criminal offense.|

---

#### **Medical Certification for ESI/Labor Claims**

The sources provide specific proformas for certificates issued by a medical practitioner under such acts.

**1. Sickness Certificate Contents:**

- Name, age, and residence of the employee.
- Specific identification marks (to prevent impersonation).
- Nature of the illness.
- The exact period for which rest is recommended (from date to date).
- Doctor's signature, designation, and office seal.

**2. Fitness Certificate Contents:**

- Certified statement that the individual has recovered from their illness.
- Statement that the person is now "fit to resume duty" from a specific date.
- Patient’s signature or thumb impression for verification.

---

#### **Maintenance of Medical Records**

★ **Important Exam Point: Rules for RMPs**

- **Duration:** For inpatients, health records must be maintained for **3 years** in the prescribed proforma.
- **Access:** If a patient or their relatives ask for the record, it must be provided within **72 hours** (under RTI/NMC guidelines).
- **Confidentiality:** Records are the property of the hospital/doctor but are held in a fiduciary relationship.

---

#### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Documentary Evidence:** Medical records are the backbone of ESI and Workman's Compensation claims.
- ★ **False Certification:** Issuing a false certificate is "infamous conduct" and punishable under **Section 234 BNS**.
- ★ **72 Hours:** The legal time limit to provide medical records upon request.
- ★ **Expert Witness:** A doctor testifying in a labor court acts as an expert witness under **Section 39 BSA**.

---

#### **Viva Questions**

1. **What is the punishment for a doctor who issues a false fitness certificate?**
    - _Ans:_ It amounts to professional misconduct (erasure of name from register) and is punishable under **Section 234 BNS**.
2. **How long should a doctor keep the OPD records of an ESI patient?**
    - _Ans:_ According to Ministry guidelines, OPD records should be kept for **3 years**, and medico-legal registers for **10 years**.
3. **What is a "suppositious child" in the context of inheritance/insurance?**
    - _Ans:_ When a woman pretends pregnancy and brings forth a child to pass it off as her own to obtain a share in property or benefits.

---

#### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **ESI Act:** Focuses on sickness, maternity, and employment injury benefits.
- **Doctor's Role:** Maintenance of records and issuance of valid Sickness/Fitness certificates.
- **Legal Trap:** False certificates lead to penal erasure and prosecution under **BNS 234**.
- **Evidence:** Medical records are secondary evidence but carry high weight in labor courts.
