### **Bioethics: Primary Principles**

---

#### **Basic Concepts**

**Definition**  
**Bioethics** is an area of philosophy which concerns ethical issues in applied biology and medicine. It involves a set of moral principles that guide medical practitioners in their dealings with patients, society, and each other.

**Introduction**  
The term ‘bioethics’ was first used by Potter in 1970. While medical ethics focuses narrowly on clinical obligations and patient interests, bioethics is more extensive and societal-oriented, covering fields like stem cell research, organ transplantation, and human experimentation.

**Objectives**

- To ensure the protection of the dignity, rights, safety, and well-being of patients and research participants.
- To provide a framework for evaluating the merits and demerits of new biomedical technologies.

---

#### **The Four Primary Principles of Bioethics**

★ **Important Exam Point: These are also known as the "Big Four" principles.**

In medical practice, four basic principles are generally recognized to evaluate the ethics of a situation:

|**Principle**|**Scientific Term**|**Simple Explanation**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**1. Autonomy**|Self-determination|The patient has the right to choose their own treatment and make decisions about their health without coercion.|
|**2. Beneficence**|Promotion of good|The doctor should act in the "best interest" of the patient to promote their well-being.|
|**3. Non-maleficence**|"Do no harm"|The doctor should not intentionally create harm or injury to the patient (_Primum non nocere_).|
|**4. Justice**|Fairness/Equity|Treating all patients equally and fairly, ensuring a fair distribution of medical resources regardless of gender, race, or religion.|

---

#### **Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Role in Criminal Investigation:** Ethical principles like "autonomy" are the basis for legal consent. Procedures performed without valid consent can lead to charges of assault under **Section 131 BNS**.
- **Role in Court Proceedings:** Courts use the "Bolam Test" or "Bolitho Test" to assess if a doctor followed standard ethical and clinical guidelines in negligence cases.
- **Importance for Medical Practitioners:** Adhering to these principles prevents professional misconduct ("infamous conduct"), which can lead to the erasure of a doctor's name from the medical register.

---

#### **Relevant Law (BNS, 2023)**

The principles of bioethics are reflected in the laws governing **Consent** and **Medical Negligence**:

|**Act**|**Section**|**Medico-Legal Relevance**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**BNS**|**25**|**Consent (>18 years):** Consent given for an act not intended to cause death or grievous hurt is valid.|
|**BNS**|**26**|**Good Faith:** Acts done in good faith for a person's benefit with consent are not offences.|
|**BNS**|**28**|**Invalid Consent:** Consent obtained through fear, misconception, or from a person of unsound mind/intoxicated is invalid.|
|**BNS**|**30**|**Emergency Exception:** No consent is required for life-saving procedures in an emergency.|
|**BNS**|**106**|**Negligence:** Punishment for causing death by a rash or negligent act (up to 2 years).|

---

#### **Advanced Ethical Principles (Research & Human Subjects)**

In addition to the primary clinical principles, the **ICMR (Indian Council of Medical Research)** guidelines for research involving human participants include:

1. **Principle of Essentiality:** Research must be absolutely necessary for the advancement of knowledge.
2. **Principle of Voluntariness:** Participants must have the right to agree or withdraw from research without discrimination.
3. **Principle of Non-exploitation:** Research should not create social or historic divisions and must ensure participants are treated fairly.
4. **Principle of Risk Minimization:** Every effort must be made to ensure risks are kept minimal.

#### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Primum Non Nocere:** The latin phrase for "First, do no harm" (Non-maleficence).
- ★ **Autonomy:** Is the legal basis for **Informed Consent**.
- ★ **Distributive Justice:** Ensuring the burdens and benefits of treatments are shared equally in society.
- ★ **Nuremberg Code:** A set of 10 ethical principles formulated in 1947 following unethical human experimentation during WWII.

#### **Viva Questions**

1. **What is the difference between Beneficence and Non-maleficence?**
    - _Ans:_ Beneficence means actively doing good for the patient, while non-maleficence means ensuring you do not cause them harm.
2. **Can a doctor treat a patient without consent?**
    - _Ans:_ Under **Section 30 BNS**, a doctor can treat a patient without consent only in life-threatening emergency situations.
3. **Which declaration provides guidelines for medical professionals against torture?**
    - _Ans:_ The **Declaration of Tokyo** (1975).

### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **Bioethics Principles:** Autonomy (Choice), Beneficence (Good), Non-maleficence (No harm), Justice (Fairness).
- **Key Latin Phrase:** _Primum non nocere_ (Non-maleficence).
- **Legal Framework:** BNS Sections 25–30 (Consent) and Section 106 (Negligence).
- **Misconduct:** Violation of ethics leads to "Penal Erasure" (removal from medical register).
