[[Toxicology]]
### **Drug Dependence and Substance Abuse**

---

#### **Basic Concepts**

**Definition**  
**Drug Dependence** is a state, arising from repeated administration of a drug on a periodic or continuous basis, characterized by a compulsion to take the drug to experience its psychic effects and sometimes to avoid the discomfort of its absence.

**Introduction**  
Substance use disorders involve mental and behavioral disturbances developed as a result of using psychoactive substances, including medications or specific repetitive rewarding behaviors. These substances primarily act on the **Central Nervous System (CNS)** and are often referred to as **Neurotics**.

**Key Principles of Dependence:**

1. **Tolerance:** A state where the same dose no longer produces the original effect, requiring an increase in dosage.
2. **Withdrawal Syndrome:** A cluster of physical and psychological symptoms occurring when the drug is suddenly stopped (e.g., 'the shakes' in alcoholics).
3. **Psychic Dependence:** A mental craving for the drug's effects.
4. **Physical Dependence:** An adaptive state where the body requires the drug to maintain physiological equilibrium.

#### **Classification of Commonly Abused Drugs**

Based on their action and chemical nature, these are classified as follows:

|**Category**|**Action**|**Examples**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Somniferous**|Sleep-inducing narcotics|Morphine, Heroin, Pethidine, Opium|

|**Stimulants**|CNS excitation|Amphetamines, Cocaine|

#### **Medico-Legal Importance**

★ **Important Exam Point: Role in Criminality**

- **Crime & Violence:** Dependence often leads to crimes committed to procure the drug or crimes committed under the drug's influence (e.g., Homicidal mania or "Run-amok" in cannabis).
- **Professional Misconduct:** A medical practitioner suffering from drug addiction or alcoholism can be charged with "infamous conduct" by the State Medical Council, leading to the erasure of their name from the medical register.
- **Sexual Offenses:** Drugs like barbiturates and benzodiazepines are used as "date rape drugs" due to their disinhibiting effects.
- **Smuggling:** "Body Packers" or "Mules" ingest wrapped packets of drugs (cocaine/heroin) for international transport; packet rupture leads to lethal toxicity.

#### **Relevant Law (BNS, BNSS, & NDPS, 2023)**

University examinations require the application of the new 2023 criminal codes alongside the NDPS Act.

|**Act**|**Section**|**Medico-Legal Significance**|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**NDPS Act, 1985**|—|Regulates the cultivation, production, possession, and sale of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances.|
|**BNS, 2023**|**22**|**Criminal Responsibility:** A person is not responsible for an act if, due to involuntary intoxication, they were unable to know the nature of the act.|
|**BNS, 2023**|**24**|**Voluntary Intoxication:** Generally not an excuse for committing a crime.|
|**BNSS, 2023**|**33**|**Mandatory Reporting:** Medical officers must inform the police of any suspected poisoning or overdose case.|
|**BNSS, 2023**|**51/52**|**Medical Examination:** Police can request a medical officer to examine an arrested person (even using force) to detect drugs/evidence.|

**Punishment under NDPS Act (Examples):**

- **Small Quantity** (e.g., Cocaine < 2g): Up to 6 months imprisonment or ₹10,000 fine.
- **Commercial Quantity** (e.g., Cocaine > 100g): 10–20 years rigorous imprisonment and heavy fines.

#### **Clinical Features of Major Dependence Syndromes**

**1. Opioid Dependence (Morphinism)**

- **Triad:** Pin-point pupils (miosis), respiratory depression, and coma.
- **Complications:** Needle tracks, abscesses, and risk of HIV/Hepatitis from IV use.

**2. Cocainism (Cocainomania)**

- **Magnan’s Syndrome:** A tactile hallucination where the addict feels insects crawling under the skin ("Cocaine Bugs").
- **Physical signs:** Black tongue and teeth, perforated nasal septum (from snorting).

**3. Alcoholism**

- **Delirium Tremens:** An acute attack of insanity seen 2–4 days after withdrawal; features include "delirium of horrors" and tremors.
- **Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome:** Thiamine deficiency causing memory loss, ataxia, and ophthalmoparesis.

**4. Cannabis Toxicity**

- **Run-amok:** A homicidal mania where the individual kills indiscriminately before attempting suicide.
- **Signs:** "Bloodshot" eyes (conjunctival injection) and increased appetite ("the munchies").

#### **Management of Drug Dependence**

1. **Stabilization (ABCD):** Maintain Airway, Breathing, Circulation, and provide Drugs for immediate life-support.
2. **Decontamination:** Gastric lavage (if ingested within 1 hour) using 1:5000 $KMnO_4$.
3. **Specific Antidotes:**
    - **Naloxone:** For opioid overdose (0.4–2 mg IV/IM).
    - **Flumazenil:** For benzodiazepine overdose.
    - **Physostigmine:** For Dhatura/Anticholinergic poisoning.
4. **Aversion Therapy:** Using drugs like **Disulfiram (Antabuse)** for alcoholics to produce unpleasant reactions (aldehyde syndrome) upon alcohol intake.
5. **Psychiatric Support:** Counselling, cognitive behavioral therapy, and rehabilitation.

#### **High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **"In Vino Veritas":** "In wine there is truth"—referring to the loss of inhibition in the stage of excitement in alcohol.
- ★ **CAGE Questionnaire:** Used for rapid screening of alcoholism (Cut down, Annoyed, Guilty, Eye-opener).
- ★ **Chasing the Dragon:** Inhaling heroin vapors heated on aluminum foil.
- ★ **Mellanby Effect:** Behavioral impairment is more severe when the blood alcohol level is rising than when it is falling.

#### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **Definitions:** Tolerance (need more), Dependence (need always), Withdrawal (pain if stopped).
- **Key Law:** NDPS Act 1985; Section 33 BNSS (Doctor's duty to report).
- **Addiction Signs:** Cocaine bugs (Magnan's), Pin-point pupils (Opioids), Run-amok (Cannabis), Delirium tremens (Alcohol).
- **Treatment:** Naloxone (Opioids), Disulfiram (Alcohol), Physostigmine (Dhatura).
