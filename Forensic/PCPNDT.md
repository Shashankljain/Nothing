[[Laws]]
### **Pre-conception and Prenatal Diagnostic Techniques (PCPNDT) Act, 1994**

---

#### **1. Basic Concepts**

**Definition**  
The **PCPNDT Act** is a federal legislation in India enacted to prohibit **sex selection** before or after conception and to regulate prenatal diagnostic techniques to prevent their misuse for **female feticide**.

**Introduction**

- **Original Act:** Enacted in **1994** as the PNDT Act.
- **Renaming/Amendment:** Following an amendment in **2003**, it was renamed the **Pre-conception and Prenatal Diagnostic Techniques (Prohibition of Sex Selection) Act**.
- **Purpose:** To stop the declining sex ratio in India caused by the illegal termination of female fetuses.

**Objectives**

- To prohibit sex selection before or after conception.
- To regulate the use of prenatal diagnostic techniques (like ultrasound) only for detecting specific medical conditions or genetic abnormalities.
- To prevent the communication of the sex of the fetus to the pregnant woman or her relatives.

**Principles of Regulation**  
No person shall conduct prenatal diagnostic techniques unless they are registered under the Act, and the tests must only be performed for the purposes listed in the law.

---

#### **2. Medico-Legal Importance**

- **Role in Criminal Investigation:** Provides the legal basis to raid unregistered clinics and prosecute doctors or individuals involved in sex determination.
- **Importance for Medical Practitioners:** Doctors must maintain meticulous paperwork (e.g., **Form F**). Even a clerical error in the paperwork is treated as a major offence under the Act.
- **Civil Rights:** Ensures the right to life for the girl child by preventing discriminatory abortion.
- **Professional Conduct:** Violation of this Act leads to the **erasure of the doctor's name** from the State Medical Register.

★ **Important Exam Point:** Every incomplete or erroneous entry in paperwork regarding sex determination is treated as a **punishable offence**.

#### **4. Principles and Indications (Clause 2 & 3)**

**Clause 2: Medical Purposes**  
Prenatal diagnostic techniques can **only** be used to detect the following:

1. **Chromosomal abnormalities**.
2. **Genetic metabolic diseases**.
3. **Hemoglobinopathies** (e.g., Thalassemia).
4. **Sex-linked genetic diseases**.
5. **Congenital anomalies**.

**Clause 3: Eligibility Criteria**  
Tests should only be conducted if the pregnant woman satisfies at least one of these conditions:

- Age is **$\ge$ 35 years**.
- Has a history of **two or more** spontaneous abortions or fetal loss.
- Has been exposed to potentially teratogenic agents (drugs, radiation, etc.).
- The pregnant woman or her spouse has a family history of mental retardation or physical deformities.

---

#### **5. Prohibitions and Documentation**

- **No Sex Disclosure:** The person conducting the procedure must **not** communicate the sex of the fetus to the woman or her relatives by words, signs, or any other manner.
- **No Advertisements:** Clinics cannot issue any advertisements regarding facilities for prenatal determination of sex.
- **Written Consent:** Informed written consent in a language the woman understands is mandatory.
- **Form F:** It is a legal mandate to submit **Form F** along with the ultrasound report.
- **Maintenance of Records:** Indoor patient records must be kept for **3 years** (or digitized indefinitely for the future).

#### **7. High-Yield Exam Points**

- ★ **Form F:** The most critical document for a radiologist to avoid prosecution.
- ★ **35 Years:** The age limit above which prenatal testing is legally permitted for medical reasons.
- ★ **Cognizable & Non-Bailable:** The nature of offences under the PCPNDT Act.
- ★ **Renaming:** Occurred in **2003** to include "Pre-conception" (preventing sex selection even at the embryo stage).

---

#### **8. Viva Questions**

1. **Can a doctor tell the sex of the fetus if the woman has a sex-linked disease?**
    - _Ans:_ Sex can be disclosed in specific sex-linked disorders found at ultrasound (e.g., Alport syndrome) only to medical professionals, but generally, disclosure to the family remains prohibited to prevent misuse.
2. **How long must a hospital keep medical records under this act?**
    - _Ans:_ For a period of **3 years**.
3. **What is the "Two-finger test" in this context?**
    - _Ans:_ (Note: Unrelated to PCPNDT, but mentioned in sources) It is an unscientific test for rape victims, which is now prohibited.
4. **What happens if a doctor makes a spelling error on the PCPNDT forms?**
    - _Ans:_ Under the Act, any incomplete or erroneous paperwork is treated as a major offence equivalent to sex determination.

---

#### **Quick Revision Summary**

- **Act:** PCPNDT Act 1994 (amended 2003).
- **Goal:** Prevent sex selection and female feticide.
- **Forbidden:** Telling the parents if it's a boy or girl.
- **Mandatory:** **Form F** and written consent.
- **Penalty:** Up to 5 years prison and permanent loss of medical license.
- **Recent Framework:** Integrated with **BNS 2023** (Sections 88-92) regarding criminal abortion.
